race/gender/science fiction - sci-fi inventory http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2008/taxonomy/term/7/0 en Hello! http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2008/node/13 <p>Everything seems to be up and running smoothly, sweet.</p> <p>I'm interested in some type of 'science fiction inventory' -- an assessment of what a particular person is drawing on when they talk about the genre as they know it. Personally, my mother often bragged about watching Star Wars with me while I was in the womb and we had a fair amount of Star Trek (both OS and TNG). As I grew up, mobs of Jurassic Park, Star Wars, and Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtle action figures lived (and did battle) side by side in a miniature plywood house we made. My brother and I saved our allowance money for X-Men and Star Wars cards, again often playing games or setting up scenes in which to role-play them duking it out.</p> <p>Interestingly, the actual literary side of science fiction played a much smaller role in determining these interests. We had a lot of Fantasy books for bedtime stories (Redwall, anyone?) and I loved "I, Robot" and the Star Wars Rogue Squadron sort of stuff, but I think it's fair to say that by the time I was a serious reader at all, I dismissed most sci-fi as fluff ("Ender's Game" was the closest I got to rediscovering the genre, but even that felt like a guilty pleasure by the time I was reading it late in high school). At the same time, we watched Voyager every week as a family (shameful, I know) and it was definitely more from movies/video games that I really learned about 'science fiction.'</p> <p>I found such books exciting (I thought about Le Guin's "the ones who walk away from Omelas" for years after reading it in 6th grade and I loved "Handmaid's Tale" in high school), but didn't really read that much on my own. Maybe it was because I fell into the trap of not doing much reading for fun. Particularly because, when I did, it was the equivalent of educational baby-food -- Harry Potter and the like. In the reading of "canonical" fiction and other coursework, I somehow forgot that there could be *good* science fiction that was stimulating on more than a surface level (I just read "Foundation" finally over winter break and it was a major reason to want to take this course).</p> http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2008/node/13#comments sci-fi inventory Mon, 28 Jan 2008 06:45:59 +0000 Kamin 13 at http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2008
