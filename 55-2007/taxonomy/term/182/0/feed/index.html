In my Victorian/Gothic Lit class, a lot of what my prof explains about the time period and the development of the Gothic novel really seems to have been carried through to the post-Modernist period and many of the themes, such as personal history and an obsession with the past, are still prevalent in encyclopedic narratives. </p> <p>I had to read about the Victorian frame of mind on the subject of love, sex, and women, and this quote from Infinite Jest really reminded me of what I had read. "We want choice. A sense of efficaciousness and choice. To be loved by someone. To freely love who you happen to love" (423). Some Victorians believed that the institute of marriage was a negative influence on society because marriages that were the product of socio-economical incentives only increased the number of extramarital affairs. This is why many were beginning to support marriages of love. Marathe's take on the American ideal of "Pride in your work and family, and home" is also a very Victorian trait (423).</p> <p><a href="http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/node/223">read more</a></p> http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/node/223#comments marathe and steeply victorians Sat, 24 Mar 2007 08:44:21 +0000 sarawrs 223 at http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007
