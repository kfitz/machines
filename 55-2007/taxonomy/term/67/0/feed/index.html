The Big Novel - lord of the flies http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/taxonomy/term/67/0 en Lor d of the Flies, Pigs, and Zits http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/node/75 <p>When I read the section where Slothrop dresses up like a pig, I immediately thought of Golding's Lord of the Flies. I don't know if Pynchon meant to allude to that book, but it's just something that I happened to take notice of. Slothrop, I felt, represented that little fat kid, Piggy, from Lord of the Flies. The children "prod his stomach," declare him the "fattest man in the world," and ultimately ask him to wear a pig costume (578). I was half expecting them to hunt him down and stick his head on a pole or something! </p> <p>This section also made me wonder, "Why a PIG?" I don't have a companion, so I don't know if this is already something that is explained, but it just struck me as odd that Pynchon would go pig-crazy.
