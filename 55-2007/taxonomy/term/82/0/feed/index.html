The Big Novel - Blicero http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/taxonomy/term/82/0 en Circle of Life http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/node/88 <p>"I want to break out-- to leave this cycle of infection and death. I want to be taken in love: so taken that you and I, and death, and life, will be gathered, inseparable, into the radiance of what we would become..." (738)</p> <p>I found this entire speech of Blicero's to be not only out of character for the usually-sadistic captain, but also quite beautiful (for what it is). In the midst of whipping Gottfried, Blicero stops to rant on life, death, original and subsequent sin and love. Are these actually some of Blicero's last words- is he dying? Or just feeling his mortality? Is Blicero feeling regret for all the rockets he's helped create- all the innocent deaths he has caused?
