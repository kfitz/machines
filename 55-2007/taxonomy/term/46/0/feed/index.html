The Big Novel - Crumbling http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/taxonomy/term/46/0 en Reverse Patterns-- Backwardness of Germany http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/node/50 <p>I have been spotting many examples of the reverse order descriptions Professor Fitzpatrick talked about since one of the first days we started reading. However, I feel like this tool of describing things backwards or in reverse order really came into play on pg 379 when Pinchot describes Germany. He talks of how "inside is outside", "but outside has been brought inside and that kind of Sunday lasts all week long", and "Earth has turned over in its sleep, and the tropics are reversed". I thought this was such a creative way of describing broken down, crumbling Germany. This figurative language is simply emphasizing the backwardness of Hitler's regime and seems to be saying, in a disheartened way, that Germany has fallen.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/node/50">read more</a></p> http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/node/50#comments Crumbling Wed, 31 Jan 2007 03:35:01 +0000 Surgiomcsurgexc 50 at http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007
