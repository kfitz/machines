The Big Novel - morality http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/taxonomy/term/62/0 en Morality/Judgement http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/node/66 <p>I was struck by a passage as I was reading today. In it, Slothrop, Otto, Narrisch and Springer are walking along and the townspeople begin to crowd them, begging, when Springer pulls a gun. </p> <p>"They're hungrier today," observes Narrisch.<br /> "True," replies Springer, "but today there are fewer of them."<br /> "Wow," it occurs to Slothrop, "thats a shitty thing to say". (503)</p> <p>This is the first time in the book so far that I remember any issue of right or wrong in a society or real judgement/morality has been expressed by a character, or at least by Slothrop. Part of what makes the book somewhat interesting to me is its complete amorality to this point. It also makes the explicit sex much easier to stomach, as it occurs in a vaccuum, with only the valences that I/readers bring to the text.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/node/66">read more</a></p> http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007/node/66#comments morality Slothrop Mon, 05 Feb 2007 07:48:14 +0000 KodiakSasha 66 at http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/55-2007
