fatal pleasures

By two bird - Posted on 21 March 2007 - 12:08pm.
Tagged:
  • Infinite Jest

The discussion on Monday about what was going on with Joelle got me thinking about the idea of fatal pleasures. Joelle might be covering her beauty because it's too much, ie. fatal beauty. It's implied that that's got something to do with why the Entertainment is so fascinating. Therefore, her beauty really can actually kill someone.

This adds beauty to the list of pleasures and pursuits that Wallace is saying can be pursued to a point of no return. Beauty, entertainment, drugs, thrills. I'd say it was a commentary on American pasttimes, except the pursuit of thrills until death was from the Fauteuils Rollants, who are definitely Canadian. Maybe that one should be left off the list, as it's not an idle pleasure? If you take it off, then the list becomes a criticism of American values and what people seek to lose themselves in.

Addiction & Excess
Submitted by Stumpy on 27 March 2007 - 12:52am.

On page 471 Steeply is describing the electrode experiment to Marathe and he talks excitedly about how the rats in the experiment would push a lever until they died just so that they could have the feeling of unbelievable pleasure. This reminded mme of experiments I have read (I'm not sure if it has been discussed in this book) about rats that become addicted to cocaine (in experiments) and it gets to the point where they would rather have cocaine than food. Marathe seems skeptical of the electrode experiment, but I think Steeply made a good point regardless because if it wasn't electrodes it was something else. Addiction is everywhere in modern culture. But there's definitely something to be said for the connection between addiction and plateaus. Think about gateway drugs. At first weed is enough. But then... maybe there is a feeling better than a high from marijuana. Then maybe you want cocaine. Then heroine. Or maybe you drink tea to focus, then you move on to coffee and then adderall. The idea of infinite jest is really interesting because it's the conquering of the plateau problem.
