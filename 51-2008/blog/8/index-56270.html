A cyborg manifesto and feminism

By bfairbueller - Posted on 24 March 2008 - 8:28am.
Tagged:
  • reading response 8

A Cyborg Manifesto by Donna Haraway was certainly a very weird text.

"The cyborg is a matter of fiction and lived experience that changes what counts as women's experience in the late twentieth century."

What i understood from this is that the machine is this masculine device which is developing and being quite prominent in the twentieth century, however, with this growth lies the end of the "women's experience" because women are no longer needed for reproduction. Machines, cyborgs in this instance, can duplicate without love or sex. They are fundamentally missing the circle of life.

Weird by Today's Standards??

By bfairbueller - Posted on 6 February 2008 - 10:59pm.
Tagged:
  • communication
  • normal
  • social
  • weird
  • what?

I understand that computers are advancing our society and that new technology is being created every day. But, i must say that i don't think that all these new advancements necessarily mean the end of a far more simpler way of life. I'm not talking Amish here, but i think a college student in today's world can survive just fine without texting, AIM or Facebook. (Or its equivalent) They will certainly still have friends and go out and carry on a "normal" life, perhaps a "normal" that has been forgotten by some. Just as the Romantic Period has passed, true Romantics live on.
