Reggae music is a phenomenon that managed to emerge out of Jamaica in the early 1930's. The content of the music expresses the daily struggle of people forced by the government to live in extreme poverty and also strongly echoes the backlashes of slavery, affecting us people in the Caribbean. It is now considered a universal language that brings people together, breaking down all cultural barriers and creating a form of brotherhood among nations.

Media Structured

By the_BIG_sleep - Posted on 27 March 2008 - 12:14am.
Tagged:
  • Enzensberger
  • Jean Baudrillard
  • reading desponse 6

Of our readings this week the dialogue between Enzensberger and Baudrillard was particularly interesting. Both discuss the innate problems with our current media forms, one with an anti-capitalist superstructure bent and the other with a more general structural criticism of mediated communication. Enzensberger's dream is a utopian, unrestricted, socialized, mobilized media in which everyone is a producer, using reversible transmissions.
