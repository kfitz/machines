Turkle & Dibbell

By Cass89 - Posted on 30 March 2008 - 1:36pm.
Tagged:
  • Reading Response 9

There are many questions to be asked about identity online. No matter who we are in real life, everything is masked online. This is how children are taken advantage of and easily lured to people who pretend to be a friend but are really a foe. Dibbell's article made me think about identity in general. Everyone has a separate identity, separate social security number, but we are all apart of the human race. No matter how jobs rank, no one is truly the boss of another individual or higher in any way. Every single person has the capability to do anything and only certain people make the choice to be dangerous. Because anyone is capable of anything, does that mean we're all the same?

I think that everyone has the same quality's and people subconsciously choose which quality's fit them the best to create who they are. In Turkle's article, she mentions a man reinventing himself online to be a suave sophisticated guy who wins over girls. Though he knows he could change, he'd worry about what others would say. If only he could project the confidence he has online in real life, he would be the gentleman he knew he could be. No matter how drastically he can alter himself online or in real life, that nerd will always be with him.

Turkle makes the same point in the beginning about not only being ONE "Sherry Turkle" in the world. Though there are many Sherry Turkle's, each one is different and has their own speciality. Like the Sherry Turkle who lived in Paris and studied poststructuralism or the professor who mentored MIT students for 20 years. She believes we are all linked together digitally through the internet. "the internet links millions of people in new spaces that are changing the way we think and the way we form our communities."

The fact that people are creating these alter-ego people to be online is really no surprise considering our society today.

Our society today, asks for a certain body type as well as personality in order to "fit in." Simply by analyzing our magazines and TV shows and movies this type of person is revealed. For girls this means, skinny, fit, tan, and longish hair. For guys this means suave, dark hair, tale, tan, masculine etc.

A perfect example of the outcome and backlash of this is Second Life. One just has to look around to understand that everyone looks very similar. And it is this image of perfection that simply cannot be achieved in real life which is forcing people to seek it in dimensions which allow for it- i.e. the internet.
