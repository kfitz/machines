The Long Tail

By Cass89 - Posted on 27 January 2008 - 5:43pm.
Tagged:
  • reading response 1

The thing I found most interesting about 'The Long Tail' was the fact that teenagers have no idea that stealing music is ruining the music business. Each individual is quickly decreasing music's mass production. It's a scary thought that between itunes and stealing music, America has put music stores such as Sam Goody and Tower Records out of business. It doesn't even occur to most people that they are stealing music. No one realizes that we are not only doing something illegal, but also insulting the music business. It's as if music isn't even worth our time or money. Bands work extremely hard to put their songs out there in the music business, spending hours recording their music. We simply throw all of that away with one simply click and no money charged. Instead of bands getting the money that they earned, they receive nothing for their hard work.

As the radio keeps running, the people of America decide what they want to hear. With itunes, there's a "Listeners have also bought.." list. When clicking on a song, a small list appears at the corner of the screen with artists that are like the song you clicked on. Through this, underground bands are discovered and made popular. We are in more control as to what becomes popular than the radio. Netflix, known as the itunes of movies, has a "Watchers also bought" list as well. It's cheaper than running out to a blockbuster, but it's actually slower. Though it takes a day or so for it to arrive, it's used more than blockbuster because of the price.

"So free has a cost: the psychological value of convenience. This is the "not worth it" moment where the wallet opens." When it comes down to it, it's all about convenience. People download music because it's fast and easy. People steal music because it's completely free.
By offering fair pricing, ease of use, and consistent quality, you can compete with free." I completely disagree with this statement. No matter how bad an object is, if it's free, it attracts more customers. When McDonalds came out with ice cream cones, it made a lot of customers sick because of the lactose in it. No one was buying the ice cream, so McDonald's decided to make it free. Even thought it made people sick, more people bought it than ever before.
