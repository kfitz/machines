final proposal

By miscellaneous - Posted on 26 March 2008 - 3:52am.
Tagged:
  • term project proposal

For my term project, I would like to create a computer game along the lines of those created by Persuasive Games. Persuasive Games develops video games that are entertaining but make a point. Its clients range from Cold Stone Creamery, Inc. to CNN.com. The genres of video games they focus on include advergaming, newsgaming, edutainment, etc. I am interested in how various organizations use games as a means of communicating their message. People can learn a lot of information about a specific issue or company simply from playing a game. For example, Cold Stone commissioned a game to train its employees. I'm thinking of creating a game related to our consumerist society. Brand companies often advertise their products to children through fun computer games. A child exploring Treasure Island on Frootloops.com may have a sudden desire to eat Froot Loops the next morning for breakfast. It would be interesting to analyze the effect of advergaming on youth. I want to look at how various food and clothing organizations are using new mediums to spread and share information. I would compare the games developed by these big-name companies and try to determine their appeal. My creative project will tie into the readings about game studies in April. I want to create a website that advertises a product of my invention and includes a game for people to play. I would definitely need our class' input for my website so I can determine what's effective or ineffective. My biggest challenge is probably building a computer game that will make people want to buy my product. I will probably use a program like Game Maker to help me. If anyone has suggestions on designing games or the contents of my game, please comment!

thoughts on my project
Submitted by miscellaneous on 6 April 2008 - 12:41pm.

I'm thinking of focusing my project on food advertising, specifically how food companies try to sell unhealthy food to kids. A recent article found that 91% of kid food ads promote high-fat, high-sugar, high-salt, or low-nutrient products. I want to create a website that promotes a sugary cereal with a game that appeals to children. The purpose of my website is to show how easy it is to tempt a child to eat unhealthy food. Companies can use online games to promote any message. Unfortunately, food companies often encourage children to eat junk food. My appendix would compare several cereal brands and how they appeal to kids through advergaming. I hope to get a copy of Ian Bogost' Persuasive Games and use it in my analysis of the power of online games on youth.
