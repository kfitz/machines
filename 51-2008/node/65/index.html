Weird by Today's Standards??

By bfairbueller - Posted on 6 February 2008 - 10:59pm.
Tagged:
  • communication
  • normal
  • social
  • weird
  • what?

I understand that computers are advancing our society and that new technology is being created every day. But, i must say that i don't think that all these new advancements necessarily mean the end of a far more simpler way of life. I'm not talking Amish here, but i think a college student in today's world can survive just fine without texting, AIM or Facebook. (Or its equivalent) They will certainly still have friends and go out and carry on a "normal" life, perhaps a "normal" that has been forgotten by some. Just as the Romantic Period has passed, true Romantics live on. And if you don't believe this then you simply haven't opened your eyes. I must say that the use of letters has not passed as well. It is certainly more sincere and thought out than any internet message and are still fully appreciated by most i must say. Sure it is a choice to choose letters over email or Facebook message but that is certainly a choice that some still do prefer.
Now i want to make another point and that is that i have absolutely nothing against Facebook or texting or their equivalent. In fact i certainly use all of the above but i simply must state that i DO NOT depend on them. They make communicating easier at times, but i wish to live my life, what to me, seems more sincere and earnest. So, i try to refrain from the electronic way to communicate but rather choose other means that show value and depth.
This is all just my opinion on the matter and please don't be offended because that is not the point of me writing this but rather simply to point out that other lifestyles exist even on this college campus that perhaps do not convert to this "new media" way of life.

I completely agree with you that cell phones, AIM, facebook, and all other forms of new social phenomenon are not necessary in today's world. However, they have been accepted and embraced by society at large, and have therefore had a large impact on the world.

In class you asserted that the computer era did not constitute a revolution. If we think back a good 50 or 60 years though, to the dawning of television, we find a similar situation. Suddenly people around the world felt "connected" to others via an electronic machine. People could see and hear things that were going on on the other side of the world. It took a few years, but eventually it had a profound impact on society. News broadcasts could go on TV, as could advertisements and presidential campaigns. I think it would be hard to argue that television did not create a revolution, because even if you disregard the social impacts, the mental state of "being connected" so deeply affected people that tey began to look at the world in a different light. We are experiencing the same thing with computers. They are changing the way we interpret the world and the events happening around us. They are working behind the scenes in every aspect of life. What more could you ask for in a revolution?
