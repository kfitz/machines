MTV and economics

By bfairbueller - Posted on 27 January 2008 - 4:18pm.
Tagged:
  • demand
  • economics
  • matching
  • MTV
  • music
  • reading response 1
  • supply

"For too long we've been suffering the tyranny of lowest-common-denominator fare, subjected to brain-dead summer blockbusters and manufactured pop. Why? Economics. Many of our assumptions about popular taste are actually artifacts of poor supply-and-demand matching - a market response to inefficient distribution." (chris anderson)

After reading this paragraph i really started to wonder...how does MTV do their supply and demand matching. Because honestly, who really liked that last Britney Spears hit "Gimme More"?? That song was god awful, but MTV seemed to enjoy playing it all the time on their morning videos. Talk about "manufactured pop."

Talking further about MTV, how is it that MTV consists of basically every kind of entertainment, but music?? These days MTV seems to be compiled from various reality TV shows that all seem basically the same. For example The Hills and Laguna Beach... I mean what?? Who cares about these high schoolers and college kids lives? I don't know them, so why would I?

My point is that the supply and demand matching seems very wrong. Maybe one of these days music television will actually consist of music, but who knows. For now it just seems a notion of the past.

good question

So is it really the public who is demanding this music or rather MTV who is feeding them their now "favorite" music?

This is a really good question -- to what extent are audience demands responsible for what we see in the mainstream media (who always say "hey, we're only giving you what you want!"), and to what extent are we being conditioned to want the things that the mainstream media is giving us?

Even more: how are the mainstream media being forced to change by the fact that we can now find the thing we actually want on the internet?
