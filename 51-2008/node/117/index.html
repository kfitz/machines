Midterm Proposal

By shimla2901 - Posted on 21 February 2008 - 9:06pm.
Tagged:
  • hypertext
  • Proposal
  • wikipedia

For my midterm project, I'd like to analyze the structure of wikipedia. Both Nelson and Bush had ideas about software with a structure like that of Wikipedia, and I would examine how Wikipedia both approaches their respective visions, yet is also different from each. Furthermore, I would examine why Wikipedia might be separate from the supposably optimal models that these scientists foresaw. Could Wikipedia be improved along the lines they suggest? Or, is the structure of Wikipedia actually more practical than what they wrote about?

Some points I hope to argue are that there is a benefit in the simplicity of wikipedia's structure, and that the structure of our minds works in a similar way to the somewhat hierarchical of wikipedia. This last point I have only read about, and if I use it I would want to find articles on it. At the same time though, wikipedia would not give us articles as custom made to our preferences as Nelson's hypertext would, and it does not allow us to create "trails" like Bush's memex. Yet I'm not sure that we would actually want either of these functions (see my last reading response for why I think we would not want it to be as customizable). If I need a concrete thesis, it would be that wikipedia evolved to be in the form that it is currently in because we as readers enjoy that more than we would like Nelson's or Bush's vision.

I'm still not completely sure how the structure of my project would work, but ideally I'd like to make it so that part of it would look like wikipedia, and for another part I would try to approximate what either Bush or Nelson suggested. In that way the structure of my project, in addition to the writing, would be contrasting the different models. However I'm not sure if I have the technical know-how to pull this off. I will believe I will probably make my project look like wikipedia, and through that I would hope to show its benefits and shortcomings. As for how this would work, I would have a wikipedia-style article on every topic I want to cover, and it would have links to other related articles. For example, I would write one article about that would be a summary of Nelson's idea of hypertext, and it would have links to the text of Nelson's origional article, the wikipedia entry on hypertext, and other relevant articles on the subject.

Anyone who reads this post, please critique my ideas as I would love to have your input.

interesting proposal
Submitted by KF on 24 February 2008 - 10:31am.

On the formal part: you might consider using a wiki for your project; free wiki hosting is available from services like PBWiki. I like the idea of attempting to mimic wikipedia in the presentation of your paper, but this will of course necessitate a good bit of work.

On the ideas: I like the idea of reading Wikipedia through the lenses of Bush and Nelson's proposals, which might give you a way to think, from Nelson's point of view, about the creation of the project as a networked knowledge structure, and from Bush's point of view, about how we navigate and interact with that knowledge structure. Obviously the goals of these three projects are different, and you'll need to find ways to acknowledge and explore that, but I'll look forward to seeing what your analysis suggests...
