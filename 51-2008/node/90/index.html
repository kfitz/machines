Defining Tools i.e. "the computer"

In response to the two articles that we discussed today in class...

In order to understand the true role a computer holds I feel we must first define and categorize two separate means of tools.

First I think there are tools that serve to help further our understanding. They do not understand for use but rather hold and contain the basics, which we already know, in order to allow us to advance more easily. Key being more easily because we could still advance without these tools, but the tools just serve to make that advance easier. For example take a calculator. We all know how to add, subtract, multiply, and divide, but having a calculator makes this simple computation faster and easier for our minds, because essentially we don't have to think. In reference to the advance with the use of a calculator I bring up the subject of calculus. When first learning how to integrate and take derivatives one does not understand how to do the calculations on a calculator so you write everything down and do it by hand. The slow and simple calculations of addition and subtraction you no longer want to spend time on because it is a greater problem that you are working on. Now once you become comfortable with this new calculus you begin to plug those calculations into the calculator and you move onto more complex problems.

The second category would be tools that "do" for us. For example a vacuum because it cleans for us, all we have to do is push a bottom, but by vacuuming we aren't going to "advance" as cleaners. The job is done and the floor is clean. End of story. We are not coming to a higher level of being from vacuuming the floor.

Now looking back to the computer believe it can be seen as the first category for the most part because it enables us to do more and advance ourselves.

I disagree... kind of
Submitted by Towelie on 17 February 2008 - 11:02pm.

I'm going to have to disagree with your analogy that there are two distinct types of machines out there. Although in the terms of this analogy it may be a stretch but vacuum cleaners and calculators are essentially helping us in the same manner. Since calculus is possible by hand, using a calculator is simply just speeding up the process in the same way that using a vacuum speeds up the process of cleaning by hand. I do agree with you that computers may be in a league of their own because they can solve some equations that it would take an eternity to solve.
