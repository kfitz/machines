As I've stated before, the lines of reality are blurred when talking about the online world.

Here is a perfect example.

A MMORPG, massively multiplayer online roleplaying game, is a place where you act as a character, gain experience, socialize with other players and fight bosses to gain equipment etc. Well people here can act as the other sex, cross dressing is everywhere, and well, you can even get married in some games.

The point I'm trying to make here is that, a lot of people in my opinion take online gaming FAR too seriously.

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0TSGUf1xbF8

The link leads to a FUNERAL held ONLINE on a game called World of Warcraft. Apparently the player had died in real life, and somehow, they are attempting to have a funeral online. However, another group on the same game, decided it would be hilarious to break up and kill everyone at the funeral.

I think that holding a funeral online is wrong.

Anyhow, people have met other people through online gaming and actually have gotten married. Youtube has quite a few cases of that, as well as other online gaming.

Anyhow, it is scary how people can get sucked into this alternate reality. It's much like a drug addiction I think.
