intro to digital media studies - Nelson http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/51-2008/taxonomy/term/124/0 en Why don't we use Nelson's "hypertext"? http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/51-2008/node/95 <p>With all of the computer software we have today, in addition to the internet, Nelson probably wonders why we don't currently use his model of hypertext. Although the internet may seem similar to his hypertext on the surface, he writes that hypertext would be "a body of written or pictorial material interconnected in such a complex way that it could not be presented on paper." Most informative websites on the internet seem to me to be a way of linking separate pages; they more like the table of contents in a book than a way of representing information in a radically new way.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/51-2008/node/95">read more</a></p> http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/51-2008/node/95#comments hypertext Nelson reading response 4 Mon, 18 Feb 2008 01:57:44 +0000 shimla2901 95 at http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net/51-2008
