The true death of an author is censorship

Submitted by revive on 6 September 2006 - 6:14pm.
  • Barthes
  • The Death of the Author

"...the birth of the reader must be requited by the death of the Author" (Barthes 55). I believe that Barthes is being way too naive here with this statement. The truth death of an author figuratively speaking is when they are censored and literally speaking when they are put to death for what they write. When an author is censored, they die for their authorship which exists and is created for the sake of their readership in the hopes and dreams that what the author writes can plant seeds of new ideas and truths for the readership. It is a delicate and a beautiful relationship that has endured history and which has allowed for the creation of countless books. To say that "the birth of the reader must be requited by the death of the Author" is denying the menacingly reality of censorship and punishment by governing bodies who will do what it takes to control what the reader reads.

I remember that a cartoonist by the name of Ted Rall did a piece where he criticized the widows of 9/11 and the war on terror as having ulterior motives (mainly to profit financially from their husbands death) and immediately he was dropped from several newspapers that had syndicated him. Now my opinion as to whether what Ted Rall did was morally right or wrong is not as important as the fact that, the viewer of his comics could not longer find access to him. What that eventually means is that as the author dies, their is no "birth of the reader". Sadly, the reader dies as well.

I thought ghostwriter in his blog "The Author Lives (sort of)" brought up a really good point of interest regarding "the problem of who is allowed to write, and how certain groups of people have been excluded from the literary world" and I believed this has every power in defining the concept of authorship in the litererary world and something that Barthes overloooks. I don't know if this is where ghostwriter was going with this, but in certain countries for example North Korea or China, what people write is heavily censored and/or influenced by the doctrines of the government. It is crucial than to take in mind the political influences that directly affects censorship, how it defines authorship and then as a direct result, how the affected authorship influences the readership.

If Barthes digs a little deeper, Barthes might realize that without the root (the author), the flower (the reader) has no chance in blossoming.
