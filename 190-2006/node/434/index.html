things to admit

Submitted by neurotica on 13 November 2006 - 1:19am.
  • doujinshi
  • fan fiction
  • manga
  • this is building on DaLynziiChic's post

i do at times enjoy fan fiction, and do enjoy doujinshi. now that i've admitted that, and the fact that i heart justin timberlake on this blog, i truly feel more at ease. now with that off my chest, i can blog like i've never blogged before!! why is it hard to admit this things? afraid that people will judge you? well what's so bad about it? why is it considered being dorky and nerdy (because you waste a lot of time immersed in worlds that are fictional)? and what's so wrong with being dorky and nerdy? love it! embrace it!

what's interesting with this, is that unlike fan fiction, i think that doujinshi is bought and sold. A lot of the issues that make fan fiction interesting is its wide availability through the internet (and the fact that it's free and anyone can do it or read it). It is able to exist that way since it doesn't make a profit, and therefore steers clear from copyright issues. (though ms. anne rice doesn't like fan fictions. well poo poo to her too!) however, with doujinshi it is an actual physical product that you can buy (to my understanding). when it's on the internet, it's often scanned versions, so it's not like it was originally conceived to be on the interent. Something else that makes doujinshi different, is that though many amateur artists do it as opposed to professional ones (though some continue to do doujinshi, only under a different pseudonym), some do end up finding professional success in the end, creating their own original manga. Making doujinshi becomes a sort of training ground before making your own stuff. Since much of the educational system we've been indoctrinated in emphasizes training in reading or writing, as opposed to drawing or other types of art, this has made it so pretty much anyone can write fanfiction, that's readable and understandable to others, as opposed to creating doujinshi, which needs an amount of skills in an area, many people are lacking in. How does the same motivation of people wanting to mess with and create stories with already created characters function differently in the realm of doujinshi versus fan fiction?
