Addicted to Addiction

Submitted by achillesheel on 4 December 2006 - 12:58am.
  • intervention

I"ve just spent the last two hours watching the A&E series called Intervention - and wow it may have just become my new obsession as much as it disturbs me. I have yet to decide how I feel ethically about the show. I should explain the premise briefly, so that y'all understand the disturbing depths of the concept. They find someone who agrees to be on a show about addiction (I think that's all their told), then they have their lives taped for a few weeks or so, then they are thrust into an unexpected intervention - all under the watchful eye of the camera. You see a girl looking for tricks to turn in order to buy heroin, an ex-ceo crying in his storage unit that contains his only remaining possessions, a middle-aged alcoholic falling out of her car. It is intimate to say the least. Actually, I'm pretty sure that it is downright exploitative. At times it's almost uncomfortable to be watching these so intensely personal moments, but you can't look away at the same time.

So in an attempt to justify my watching of this show, I'm starting to find positive aspects or affects of the show. Aside from the obvious - that you see people turn their lives around with the help of people who care about them - you get a different view of addiction than I think we often find in media. Yeah, we get the don't shoot your best friend because you smoked pot PSAs, but for the most part, in my experience, addiction is glamorized in many ways. In films, even the eventual downfall is beautiful - the addict is glamorous even as he hits rock bottom. It's presented almost as a life experience that adds to the person's character or personality. And this show doesn't paint any romantic or glamorized version of addiction ( I won't call it real, because that doesn't exist in TV), but instead a painful and uncomfortable picture of addiction low points. I don't think it will change views of addiction and abuse, but it tempers some of the other media that exists about addiction.

OH MY GOD, okay, first of

Submitted by oneoutofseven on 4 December 2006 - 9:14pm.

OH MY GOD, okay, first of all, I LOVE that show-- though I agree, it's kind of a sick, sick habit. What a strange idea, too... I think my issue with this show (like most reality television shows based on previously existing circumstances (except maybe, you know, the poverty already present prior to an Extreme Home Makeover)) is its obviously exploitative nature: the people surrounding these drug addicts are so worried about their relative/friend that they'll even try exposing everything on television if it might be possible to stop their relative/friend. I can't imagine myself or anyone I know agreeing to a show like that. I wonder if they tell the addict the true nature of the show, or if they skew it toward its more documentary aspects. It's certainly an interesting proposal, and those episodes I've seen have all been successes, though I'm sure there're also a number that're not...
