Thesis Topic Ideas

Submitted by WildCherry15 on 27 September 2006 - 12:09am.
  • thesis topic

My potential thesis reader recommended that I develop 5 concrete ideas for a thesis topic that are both interesting and feasible. I should talk about them with classmates and perhaps with other profs. Then I should come back to him. So let me know what you think, peoples! Here are the ideas:

1. Further develop the idea of literary genre transformations online
a. pros:
i. very interesting/creative
ii. sort of involves nostalgia in a wayïƒ rekindling my sensations and love for how I experienced reading and school projects & then trying to recreate that for others [dioramas; Mrs. Thomas's videos (We made videos that synthesizes literature we read, involving game shows, etc.); mind maps; Mrs. Jasek's vocab. & academic decathlon (repetition and usage but in a fun way); etc.]
iii. I can continue to work on my summer project, which I have a decent start on. Also, this involves research on my family, which will sustain my interest.
b. cons:
i. sorta aloof and avant-garde
ii. not that good for networking with professionals
c. resources
i. AbuelitaRosita.com (my website I created over the summer)
ii. Hamlet on the Holodeck
iii. Scott McCloud with poetry
iv. Mind maps, etc., from SDSU website
v. Stuff from New Media class last fall (database narrative, McCloud's comics, Ambassador Hotel walk-thru)
vi. E-newletters and books from MS 196 summer course
vii. Arcade Education
viii. Educational Video Center (EVC) (could visit in New York over winter break; should also visit Columbia grad school while there)

2. Make (or at least design) an ideal supplementary class website (CSO one for UCPDI)
a. Pros:
i. Practical and useful--strong connections to education
ii. Connection to UCPDI, could lead to my thesis being viewed by important people in education
iii. Good for networking (UCPDI, Irvine conference, talking with teachers and students)
b. Cons:
i. Less personal exploration into themes of nostalgia, etc.
ii. Doesn't include my family as content
c. Resources
Ones listed in previous option, but especiallyâ€¦
i. Creating website I envisioned for the CSO project
ii. Digital History book
iii. SDSU example
iv. E-newletters (Some teachers have already done this type of thing.ïƒ  Idea of presenting outline, updating as go along, good for absent kids, with links. See report from summer reading course.)
v. Look at Pomona as a case study
1. effectiveness of Moodle/Sakai/WebCT/other course-specific sites like our blog aggregator
2. our course website/aggregator and the idea of student-submitted links and flicker-esque font sizes depending on popularity/# tags
3. maybe other MS course sites (or those of other profs)
vi. investigate other schools' usage or potential usage
1. Columbia-social work grad schoolïƒ  hub for resource accessibility (Do NCCJ or other social work groups do this already? Examine existing resources instead of just trying to create my own. And if I do create my own, embed existing features, like YouTube and that format Charles used to create NinjaTempura.com with user feedback.)
2. Preuss student-created, student-demanded web resources (Ask students what they need!)
3. ditto for charter schools that specialize in technology like High Tech High or one that Gina Lamb's class works with

3. Design one week of curriculum (for here or international English class)
a. Pros:
i. same as above but
ii. low-tech (and thus more within my technical ability to produce a product)
iii. can have people test it out and incorporate responses in thesis (psych study-esque and thus perhaps more credible as a resource for other studies)
iv. in-line with what reader prof does
v. can make supplementary book and DVD
vi. can make for if I get a Fulbright; how would I do it in a very engaging way? (think Mrs. Thomas style, as well as reader's style, and mine too obviously; can unite other thesis ideas to make innovative website)
b. Cons:
i. inherently sort of boring, drudgeryïƒ  very practicalïƒ can't explore artsy presentations so much
ii. if it were interesting, that would only be because it combined the other projects, making it very complicated and not focused enough
c. Resources:
i. Reader prof (right up his alley)
ii. Internet sites he recommended to me on foreign language learning tips

4. international policyïƒ  look at website for orgs (gov and otherwise) and see if helpful (public edu; national edu; cross-cultural edu)

5. international eduïƒ  see if other countries (one Europe, one 3rd world, one Communist) use tech in edu and how

6. my IDïƒ  develop website for ID exploration; think of MORE and Mavin's website/mag format

7. family tree softwareïƒ new idea (like AbuelitaRosita, but also G-pa's thing, FamilyTreeMaker, MyFamily.com, etc.) probably not--let software developers do that; anyway, the presentation of one's relatives is so individual that I wouldn't feel right making a standardized format.

**Right now, I think I like idea #1 (best for personal exploration) and idea #2 (best for career development, although I'm still hesitant about plunging into education after being in the classroom for 17 years.) I think I will do idea #1, but devote significant attention to reviewing the educational implications in the form I develop. Sadly, I think this means I might not be able to have the reader that I have been praising in my last 2 blogs. His methods are very practical, and I don't know how much my project will be practical. I will think more on my project and then decide before October 13. I have some teachers in mind who might be more appropriate for this project idea (Donapetry, Fitzpatrick.)
