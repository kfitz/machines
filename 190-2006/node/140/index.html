Brangelina loves Banksy...

Submitted by achillesheel on 26 September 2006 - 6:21pm.
  • banksy
  • beautiful people
  • money
  • pachyderms
  • Paris Hilton

and so do a lot of other celebs who attended the "Barely Legal" show (Keanu, Jude Law, McCauley Culkin, Xtina).
Unfortunately I didn't make it to the show so I can't comment directly, but evidently and according to LA weekly writer Linda Immediato, the audience took an ironic turn for the worse. In addition to the high star power, there were a bunch of "well-dressed sorts in expensive clothing... a handful of miscreant graffiti kids, a few tattoed rabble rousers, but by and large the crowd was college-educated gorgeous. Everywhere I turned, my ears were flooded with people praising the show: 'brilliant,' 'amazing,' 'genius,' blah blah blah, 'important.'" She thought she was in the wrong place. The scene she depicts makes my stomach turn even in print. And I know it's lame to let art be affected by the other people who like it, but I (and Linda, evidently) am affected. the simple fact that some idiot likes the same thing I might enjoy makes me have disdain for it. anyway, back to banksy. what's interesting to me is that (assuming Linda wasn't the only one with this response) is that the composition of the audience affected the actual art... It seems that Banksy's intent (whatever revolutionary social change it might be) was overshadowed or even subverted by the overwhelming prescence and acceptance of those "college-educated gorgeous." In the end, Immediato makes the genius comparison between the exhibition's reception and "the Banksy sketch of people lined up to buy T-shirts that read 'destroy capitalism.'" So maybe this is the kind of hypocritical scene Banksy craves - mainstream richies hobnobbing and talking about how "brilliant" and "important" doctoring up Paris Hilton CDs was, and all the while gawking at Christina Aguilera. Gotta love the irony.

Oh, and the prices for a piece of the proletariat show were pretty steep: prints $500, Paris CDs $1400, paintings at least $80,000 - yeah pricey. I wouldn't doubt that he does good stuff with the profits, but damn that sounds like high culture art type of prices.

But I still like Banksy I guess, even though everyone else does too.

Banksy and Brangelina sittin' in a tree...

Submitted by PureJaqassary on 26 September 2006 - 6:39pm.

I think that's precisely why he chose to hold the show in LA, because he does love hypocritical scenes like that one. I think the same applies to the expensiveness of the work, I think he revels in the thought of snobs putting down absurd amounts of money for his work so that he can continue to mock them and attempt to undermine their cultural power. Which is not to say that I think it's fully effective or that I totally agree with him.

Incidentally, I also love Banksy. He's part of the inspiration for my thesis.

PS: don't hate on Brangelina too much. They may be Hollywood movie stars, they may be shallow, self-involved idiots, but you do have admit that they make a greater effort to do good things with their noteriety than a lot of similarly rich and famous types. eg. the (undoubtley problematic) work that Angelina does for numerous charitable efforts in developing countries and their refusal to tie the knot until same-sex marriage is legalized.

K I S S I N G...

Submitted by roundmidnight on 26 September 2006 - 9:37pm.

I would have to agree with PureJ on that one. Banksy fiends on hypocrasy, and what better place than LA is there to create a "scence" around someone who doesnt want to be seen?
Your post made me think about the larger picture of art.
I used to change my preferences based on what the masses enjoyed; if they liked it, I didnt. Now I am the wiser and I think that my old view of things was absurd. MF DOOM and DangerMouse collaborated with Cartoon Network, and made an extremely successfull album, both musically and commercially. Just because I knew about them when hardly anyone was buying their albums doesnt make me any more special than some 13 year old who heard a DANGERDOOM song on the tv.

I was at the Banksy show, and I have to say that I was fighting my urges to shout at hipsters, elderly peeps, and newscasters. But then I thought, what the fuck am I getting so worked up about. Banksy is on point. Just because some fools want to pay $500 for a print, or $45,000 for a canvas doesn't take anything away from him or his art show. It is so important to realize that becasue of that art show people were inspired, regardless of their coooolnes. Banksy paints in Brixton London primarily, its crazy that LA was lucky enough to be visited by the one graffiti artist that matters.
