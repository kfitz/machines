Masturbating in the Marketplace

Submitted by DaLynziiChic on 27 November 2006 - 12:15am.
  • cynicism
  • Diogenes

In chapter 3, Vaidhyanathan (I should probably learn how to pronounce that) references two different types of cynicism. The first pertains to Diogenes (ancient Greek who would whack it in the marketplace) and is referred to as "Diogenic cynicism". According to Vaidy: "Diogenic cynicism values discipline, self-sufficiency, and rejecting the influence of social convention or cultural power" (26). The other type of cynicism is "Costanzan", referencing George Costanza of SIENFELD. Costanzan cynicism is "disengaged" and "selfish" (26). The overall impression is that Diogenic cynicism is socially responsible, while Constanzan cynicism is egotistical.

Alright, so I guess masturbating in the marketplace supports "self-sufficiency" and "rejecting the influence of social convention", but COME ON. I think it's gross. In fact, it seems to be more "disengaged" and "selfish" than socially responsible. Vaidy makes a cute remark that the Internet is like "masturbating in the marketplace" because you can jack off and shop on Amazon.com at the same time. We need to move away from this cheeky observation and back to the horror that is a guy pleasuring himself in a marketplace, possibly near the produce section. I honestly don't believe Diogenes was trying to be progressive or make a statement about social conventions. You don't masturbate in the marketplace for the same reason you don't deficate in the well. Some social conventions are for the comfort and safety of others. Diogenes was just perverted, or perhaps overly stimulated by commerce. But I fail to see him as a hero. Instead I see him as a dirty old man, sorta a cross between Jack Nicholson and post-Passion Mel Gibson.

Obviously Diogenes was a pretty funny guy, and maybe I would have liked to hang out with him in locations other than the marketplace. I just think a lot of Vaidy's validity in his cynicism argument is lost on his idolization of a perverted and horny dude, mostly because I just can't accept masturbating in the marketplace as a responsible response to social conventions.
