Farewell (unless I post more later...)

Submitted by racinian on 13 December 2006 - 6:03pm.
  • guilt

In the beginning of the semester I hated the idea of the blog. I cringed every time I opened the website and glared at the blank page in front of me. NO! I won't share my ideas with the class! I hate the idea of class participation enough, don't make me show people how poorly I write, or how little I understand some of these readings. I don't do well with social pressure.

Little by little I warmed up to the blog. I saw it as a place to rant or share my ideas, post episodes of South Park and link to fun clips and articles. It helped even more after I started posting thesis-related entries that allowed me to explore my thoughts a bit and develop my argument.

Throughout the whole process, reading other people's blog entries was infinitely more exciting than writing my own. You guys have some pretty interesting things to say, and a lot of you successfully made me feel like I had a lot of learning to do (although I know this wasn't your intention). I am really happy the comments people gave each other, it was a very supportive environment.

So, I guess I've come "full circle" with this blog. I won't be sad to see it go, but I'm definitely more open to blogging than I ever thought I would be.

Question: did anyone start blogging on another website as a result of this class? Like I said, it definitely opened my eyes to blogging, although I won't be pursuing it after this semester (it just isn't my cup of tea). Has anyone else changed their minds?

My Farewell as well

Submitted by BuildingsAndFood on 13 December 2006 - 8:32pm.

Sup racinian? You're probably not going to read this since you just signed off, but I was planning how to do my sign off earlier this afternoon while slowly being consumed by unconsciousness during Nap Time, and I thought of saying a lot of the same things that you have already articulated. Additionally, since I've tried to be an advocate of posting comments as well as blog entries in order to foster more of the community environment here, I figured it would be appropriate to sign off with a comment.

I too started off very lukewarm to the idea of the blog, but less because I don't like participating in class, rather because in participation I like to cater my comments to respond to other peoples' thoughts and reactions. The blog just felt so much more impersonal than anything else I've ever done - part of why I thought that more dialogue in the form of comments would have been conducive to writing entries. I'm also just not used to forming my rough ideas in writing; instead, I like to talk them out. It was just a different medium than I am used to.

However, like you, I have also warmed up to the idea - particularly in these last couple of weeks. Initially I was trying to respond directly to the readings and found that was not the best source of inspiration. It was also easy to get intimidated by my other classmates' observations. Now, as I've been talking more about the broader themes of the class and sharing thoughts on media and authorship in general, I've become a lot more comfortable. I also like that it feels like the blog has shrunk in size due to people stopping because they filled their quota. With fewer people, I feel like actual communication can go on.

I haven't started blogging anywhere else yet, but I hope to do so on my band's mySpace page (www.myspace.com/suavepromise) soon as I intended to at the beginning of this most recent summer. And I'm also working on starting a journal so hopefully I'll get better at doing that. I think I've gotten better at thinking analytically about media things and articulating the ideas I've had because of this. And even though I haven't hit the requirement yet, I feel like I've learned a lot from the experience, and thus feel like it's a good place to stop. I hope it stays up for a while, though, so that I can access some of the things I wrote about my thesis during Winter Break. Thanks racinian, not just for the great work on our presentation but also for posting your blog. You inadvertantly helped me with this one. Everyone take care.
