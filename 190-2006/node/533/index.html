Paltalk

Submitted by reliablesrc07 on 28 November 2006 - 4:36am.
  • paltalk
  • video social network

About paltalk from paltalk.com:

"Paltalk isn't just the latest, greatest messenger or the most diverse and vibrant chat room community - it's the ultimate online communications tool, period. Paltalk enhances the traditional instant message and chat room functionality you know and love with state-of-the-art voice and video that you'll enjoy with all your senses. That's why Paltalk is the world's most popular free voice and video chat instant messenger service.

The world's most popular voice and video chat service.

Whether you want to chat with friends or meet new ones, Paltalk is your one portal to all things talkative. Paltalk is compatible with AOL, Yahoo! and ICQ messengers, and connects you to a world of buddies. With Paltalk's easily-navigable, live voice and live video enabled chat room community, the world wide web feels more like a network of close friends. And now cyberspace really can be a good place to meet someone with Paltalk People, the best way to add more of those "special" Paltalk buddies to your list. So download Paltalk now for free to see, hear and be there with Paltalk."

Video social networking??

Yup. Paltalk president Joel Smernoff says that "Voice is ok, but it lacks richness."

What Paltalk claims to be is the merging of Myspace and YouTube. supposedly, such a visual form of social networking promotes a greater sense of "knowing" the users and thus an effective community with closer connections. I dont know if I neccessarily agree with this. It has to be taken into account that users have the ability to put a performance for the camera.

I cant see how such a tool would be convenient for example as a ay to communicate visually when located in different geographic regions. think a study abroad student with family in another country. the problem is that there are other programs that allow this. What is different is that Paltalk allows for the embedding of their video chat rooms in online communities such as MySpace.

Paltalk wants to create an actual highly functional network but i have to question whether a large enough market is out there right now. Social networks online are still rather new and adding the visual component add another very real aspect that users may not be quite ready to embrace. Many people are don't neccessarily want their identity so readily and more concretely avaiable. not to mention that in order to view peoples video feeds, the user has to pay certain fees. Users really have to want to use video to pay. and this will certainly affect the popularity of this network. I am sure there is a niche market for such a network. But in my own personal opinon, the fact that anyone who wishes to do so can obtain information so easily and in so many different forms scares me. Especially when it comes to my own personal information. I like a certain level of privacy for myself. it isnt about remaining anonymous or wanted to be deceptive, I just feel that the less access to my identity and the less people know about me is better in the long run.

I'm certainly not saying that video social networks shouldnt exist...i just have great reservations towards them for myself.

I guess only time will tell if Paltalk will take off os a separate entity as a video social network or if it will simply be an additional tool for businesses to host meetings or for Myspace users to create chat rooms.
