Blog post in which I talk about my project that no one should read

Submitted by achillesheel on 14 November 2006 - 5:06pm.
  • failing
  • paralysis
  • schizophrenia

You should stop reading now. Seriously, this is just me sorting through my head. I've always held some sort of contempt for all those super personal bloggers that just talk about how they're feeling from day to day ("my cat is sick :( and shelly called me a fat whore and boo hoo"), but I was just overcome by that impulse to vent and send it into cyberspace. And what better to rant about than that sleeping ominous giant of an anxiety-inducing undertaking. the senior project. Here's the problem, I found an idea that I like... which makes starting it that much harder. My previous thesis topics would be fairly easy to start and finish by comparison.
At the moment, I have pages and pages of ideas and scribbling, all of which adds up to nothing. And all I have are more ideas. All the time. Which is proving a big distraction. Also, I fell paralyzed by the same thing which I am trying to critique. In beginning to actually write/create the project, I just can't seem to get past the overwhelming research stage. I just keep reading more and more magazines (source material) and sinking deeper into the culture which I am trying to critique. I finally threw away a huge stack of them this morning and I swear it weighed ten pounds.
The experience of reading and being surrounded by magazine media is an incredibly overwhelming experience for me. I feel simultaneously excited about the prospect of applying all this new information to my life to improve it (whether movies or clothing or artwork or foreign travel or hunting gear reviews), and also frustrated and confused as to which to pursue, or, more often, how to pursue all of them simultaneously. Reading five different magazines is like being presented with like 100 lifestyle decisions for me. And after reading so many, I have no way to pass value (even simple aesthetic value) judgments on any of them. They all seem somehow equally great and equally valid. Which they may be, but it sure makes deciding what to eat for breakfast difficult.
