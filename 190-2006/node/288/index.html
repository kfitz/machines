potential and rejected topics (PRT): a series of blog postings - pt. 1 copyright away!

Submitted by neurotica on 24 October 2006 - 12:32am.
  • major thesis depression

Somehow, though I have a tenuous relationship with academia (not comfortable in ivory towers), while unsuccessfully trying to find one thesis topic, everything suddenly became really interesting to me. Here's a list of random topics that are interesting that I have yet to blog about, though I have no idea whether I will do any of these or not. Everything interests me but not paricularly more than anything, so I think i might settle on wikipedia (since that was what my proposal was on). What do you think my thesis should be on? Just comment on them!
:

1) Music copyright law and its impact on tv/film: With current copyright, its really expensive to get the rights to show someone else's stuff in your work, even if it's incidentally there. This is really impacting documentaries. For example, there was this case where in a documentary they were just shooting random people, and someone's phone when off and the ring tone was the Rocky theme, and the company expected the filmmakers to pay for the rights. How is this impacting filmmaking and television production? In the film, I am Sam, the featuring of Beatles song was an important part of the narrative. However, since the filmmaker couldn't afford the rights, instead she features Beatles covers, by Rufus Wainwright, Sarah Mclachlan, etc. Another interesting example are these two documentaries about Morrissey and how he has a large Latino fan base. One of them, Viva Morrissey, features a lot of music, and has been having trouble getting rights, and hasn't been widely distributed, just in festival circuits. Another film, Is It Really So Strange, works around the law by not having music at all, which is pretty interesting, the idea of a music documentary without music. Is the need to work around copyright law creating new innovations?
