Additionally, while the drink may be able to claim to be "pure" of many things that other energy drinks have (mostly sugars) this doesn't change the fact that it contains an absurd amount of caffiene, which is in fact a stimulant, on which this country has a increasingly worrisome dependence. While I'm personally pretty opposed to the criminalization of most drugs, mainly because I am opposed to the government interfering to that degree with people's personal morality, but I do think that if they are going to insist on regulating stimulants, they should really start regulating the quantity of caffiene that consumer goods can contain. I mean we are talking about a stimulant which can be fatal in high enough doses, especially to those with sensitivities, can excerbate pre-existing heart conditions, *is* habit and dependence forming, does have side-effects, and comedown effects, and which is being marketed to to a younger and younger audience. People talk about ADD and ADHD being such a problem these days and they wonder why, I'm sitting here saying "people, set boundries for your kids and stick to them, don't use the TV as a babysitter, and stop giving them so much sugar and caffiene."
