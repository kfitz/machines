related to my last post

Submitted by neurotica on 22 October 2006 - 11:34pm.
  • hamlet on the holodeck

related to the point of having a plethora of realities possible in storytelling, Murray talks about fan cultures, and how they are able to create their own alternate possibilities and readings of their favorite characters, though mediums such as fan fiction and what not. however, with fan fiction (for film or TV at least), what I always found somewhat lacking or not as satisfying, is the fact that it's not the same medium. this reminded me of what we talked about with "understanding comics", about how meaningful it was that theory on a medium was conveyed in that very medium. it can be jarring when there is that disconnect. With advances in technology, will there be more fan videos in the same vein as fan fiction? What I first thought of question, I thought of the phenomenon of star wars fan films. however, browsing through a list of them at wikipedia, many of them didn't necessarily seem like continuations or alternative versions of the starwars stories. they seemed more to be either parodies, or unique stories that happen to exist in that universe, or a way to insert themselves into those stories, as opposed to reimagining princess leia or something like that. this is pretty understandable at this point in time, being able to make images of carrie fisher to say what you want is not very accessible. film is still bound to the representations of specific individuals, which can be manipulated but not yet quite in the same way writing can do. however, a few weeks ago, I stumbled on this video on YouTube about Brokeback Mountain that reimagined the ending, so instead of tragedy the two men got married. audio from the film was used, while the visuals were CGI animation. this particular video wasn't that great. the men looked nothing like their counterparts, the animation was not that great (Very jerky movements), etc. however, this was most likely some random person who was into animation who did this. in the professional realm, cgi animation is getting more realistic, and the technology is becoming more widespread. will one day people animate their own heath ledgers and jake gyllenhaals into happy endings in video, as opposed to only relying on writing or poor visual renditions of them. if that is possible, will people just invent their own films, animate their ideal actors to play out whatever story they want. if that happens, would there be a need for the film industry. on one hand, we can definitely imagine for ourselves. however, as \massive fan cultures show, being inspired by someone else's creation can also be an effective creative agent.
