SISSYFIGHT

Submitted by DaLynziiChic on 31 October 2006 - 12:46am.
  • games
  • zimmerman

While reading Mark Zimmerman's article on the 4 Naughties, I couldn't help but wonder where I'd heard his name before. So, like any good student of my generation, I visited my friend Wiki. Wiki told me that Mr. Zimmerman is the creator of SISSYFIGHT, an online game that I've actually played before (encouraged by my mother, who says that the game designers in her office play it all day on their breaks).

Anyhow, I encourage everyone to play it.
www.sissyfight.com

The "narrative" aspect of this game is simple. You are children on a playground. The point is to "take down" the self-esteem levels of other children by "teasing", "grabbing", or "scratching". You can retaliate by "cowering" or "telling", which gets everyone (who did something bad) in trouble.

The "interactive" component is much more complex. First of all, players are allowed to design their own characters by choosing from a variety of hair-styles, faces, skin tones, and hair colors. This customization could be much more in-depth, but usually it results in a variety of different-looking children on the playground. The player also chooses a distinct name, which s/he can save for future plays. Points are saved to this name even when the player navigates away from the site, so that s/he doesn't lose what s/he has accumulated.

On the "playground", players can interact with eachother through chatting. This text appears as chat bubbles above the children's heads. Other players can team up with eachother to collectively "tease" a child, which exponentially decreases her self-esteem. However, a player can predict this and tattle on everyone. There is interactivity both between the girls on the playground AND the actual players who are talking to one another during the game. This is a pretty fun aspect, even though it results in a lot of harassment, as does most online chatting. I told everyone that I was a 12 year old girl and was instantly asked "if I was hot". Dateline, anyone?

I suppose I covered "play" a bit in the last paragraph. On to the last: "games". ....This is indeed a game, with rules and structure. The "moves" that one sissy can put on another are very restricted, but the "moves" one player can put on the other (especially through creepy chat) are endless. It is interesting how Sissyfight relies on extremely artificial avatars and yet utilizes realtime chat. The quantifiable outcome is points, which are tallied every so often and added to a list, very much like George Costanza's FROGGER.

Enough of that. Go play sissyfight. I lost.
