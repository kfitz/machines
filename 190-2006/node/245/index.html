Are blogs doomed?

Submitted by racinian on 18 October 2006 - 3:11pm.
  • blogging and YouTube

So, I was going to write about how Google is taking overe the world. A few weeks ago I had a discussion with some people in my hall about Google being Jesus, because the internet was "broken," except for Google! In any case, I got a bit caught up with Google buying YouTube but never got around to posting about it. People have posted YouTube links on here before, but I'm not sure that anyone discussed Google buying YouTube (at quite a hefty sum...)

But, just as I was going to post about it, I read this, which is slightly more relevant to our class. So, thoughts anyone? Are blogs doomed because they're bound to language? Are blogs-in-translation out of the question? And to what extend is a blog-in-translation the same as the original? While this video doesn't rely on language, it does rely on Western pop-culture references, so can it really be universal? Interesting to note that this commentary was posted in a NY Times blog...

A note on the video: it's pretty hysterical, if you're tired and overworked like me. Who is this guy, and what, someone please tell me, is an inspirational comedian? I was a bit offended that he tried to impersonate MJ dancing, it just shouldn't be done by a man wearing a tucked in Orange Crush shirt. Can't you just see him practicing in front of a mirror by himself at home? That's what makes it great for me. I bet be busts these moves out at the clubs and the ladies just go crazy.

it depends

Submitted by neurotica on 18 October 2006 - 6:45pm.

I feel like most of my replies to other posts have the title "it depends", but that's the most apt description of my opinions. anyways, I agree that a lot of the video relies on Western references that are not universally shared. however, since it has physical comedy, that's something that could be shared. someone in one culture may laugh because the guy looks funny doing a certain move, while another person may laugh because he looks funny doing a michael jackson move specifically. even though, the appeal may be universally spread, the particular appeal itself may be different from one culture to the next.

in my personal opinion, I don't think that blogs are doomed, at least not anytime soon. They just serve a different function than videos and icons. even though they may not be able to have the completely universal appeal as other videos, they do spread particular kinds of information widely within its limitations of language, etc. for exampe, the exchanges we're having in this blog for senior seminar. sure, someone who doesn't speak english may get it. however, our experiences are still so entrenched in language, how could we communicate these ideas in a way that wouldn't use language? i'm not saying it's impossible, but at this point in time, it's difficult to imagine. things like youtube and the growth of icons, at this time at least, i view as more creating a new type of communication that's global, yet it's not at a stage where it can replace the other, more limited, types of communication.
