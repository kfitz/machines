BULLY

Submitted by racinian on 2 November 2006 - 6:28pm.
  • first person

Last night, while watching South Park, I saw a preview for a new video game (for PlayStation 2) called "Bully." It looks like Harry Potter without the magic, and I just could not figure out for the life of me what the point of the game was. The preview centered on "the new kid" as he met people at Bullworth Academy (the jocks, the nerds, the bullies, the hot girls, etc.) and I'm assuming he takes up the "stop bulling the nerds" cause. In any case, I found this interesting primarily because of the article in First Person titled "Videogames of the Oppressed" which advocated for a computer/video game based on the techniques used in "Theater of the Oppressed." Basically, the game/performance involves a lot of audience interaction and actors work "undercover" in the "Forum Theater." Frasca writes, "Forums are created around a short play (5-10 minutes long), usually scripted on-site, and based on the suggestions of the participants. The scene always enacts an oppressive situation, where the protagonist has to deal with powerful characters that do not let her achieve her goals...The scene is enacted without showing a solution to the problem. After one representation, anybody in the audience can take over the role of the protagonist and suggest, through her acting, a solution that she thinks would break the oppression" (88- whew!).
Now, I have no idea how this game works, and after exploring the website I still have no idea, but if the game does deal with "solving" the problem of bullying, I wonder if this isn't a step towards the type of game that Frasca advocates. In any case, it looks pretty funny, even if I don't know how to play it.
