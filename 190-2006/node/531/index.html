Lonelygirl15

Submitted by reliablesrc07 on 28 November 2006 - 3:48am.
  • lonelygirl15
  • youtube

I dont know if all of you have heard about the youtube web series lonelygirl15....

For more info, go to the Wired Magazine article here: http://wired.com/wired/archive/14.12/lonelygirl_pr.html

or wikipedia Lonelygirl15.

Essentially, its a video series on youtube about a young girl and her video blog shot in her room. except. its totally fictional. initially, the series was assumed to be real because there was no disclaimer stating otherwise. When it was first posted, the video mocked an already popular non fictional (or as non fictional as an internet identity can get) youtube vlog (video blog). This essentially was what helped create a fanbase for it. After a few months, the creators were outed. And just how many "great" things are made popular or known by controversy, so was Lonelygirl15. It was the shock that propelled it into notoreity. And rather than being the end of the series, this was the beginning of something new.

As we all know, youtube is a fairly new medium for videomakers to put their work out there. The advancement and facilitation in internet technology, video and editing software have all made it a very real phenomenon. Anyone who has access to the internet and a camera can become a video maker. ALmost everywhere I hear people quoting popular youtube videos (such as "shoes")or i get youtube links from friends to videos they think ill enjoy. for me its celebrity gossip videos. It is exactly this increased interest and use of this video social network that has now allowed for a whole new sort of series to arise.

What Lonelygirl thrives on now is the ability for viewers to interact on a whole new level with the creation of their show. With feedback from viewers, the creator are able to gain an even more loyal fanbase. The viewers hint at what they want to see and the creators (if they feel it is pertinent to where they want to go with their story) can give them what they want. at a low cost. The creators make an interesting point in saying that the internet calls for a whole new form of series. just how tv calls for tv video and how movies call for films suitable for the movies. Each is appropriate for its medium.

I think that the ability for users to interact to such a great degree signals a change in how we perceive the internet and other mediums. we as viewers are no longer being just that. the internet has forced us to interact with eachother. and now we want to be able to interact at all levels of communication and media. But not all mediums such as tv and films (maybe to an extent snakes on a plane) affords such a possibility. but the internet defintely does. it is most likely in part due to the lower cost impplications that the internet requires. but also because we as hyperinteractive users of the internet are the blood in the internets veins. we are what keep it alive with the constant flow of information, the creation of websites, the posting of pictures and now videos.

And now with lonelygirl15 and youtube, something new is happening. People now have an even greater pool of alternate forms of entertainment from mainstream tv and movies. On the same note, more people have the ability to create videos and to gain a certain notoriety (if an audience is captivated.)

This isnt to say that TV or movies are on the outs, simply, what i think is that youtube has afforded a completely new paradigm shattering form of information dispertion.

I think it would be interesting to see what other projects the popularity of lonelygirl15 spawns.
