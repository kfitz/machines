My Blogs and Authorship

Submitted by reliablesrc07 on 10 October 2006 - 9:34pm.
  • blogging-return to writing

So I noticed that whenever I try to write my blog, or type i should say, my blog directly onto my computer, they end up being less of what I had intended and less of what Id hoped to present comes through. Everything, form information to my authorial voice is lost somewhere in translation. Maybe somewhere in my brain the supposed effiency that computers offer has made me less expressive when im using this particular medium.

So i tried somethign new, I wrote my blogs for this week on paper first, and voila. I was able to express myself more accurately My writing maynot be perfect, but i definetly think that there is a direct connection withthe return to a more organic form of hand writing that allows a less mediated me shine through.

Maybe this sounds a little off the wall, but i'm sure somewhere out there someone agrees with what i just discovered. Writing thingd downs really does give me an opportunity to really buildon my ideas. Maybe it has to do with the maleability of the hand written wrd that requires less structure. (ie notes, crossing out, highlighting, personal printing)

What do you think?

Does my Blog still remain a true "blog" of I write them down first and then type them??

haha, i gotta agree. either it is still a blog or it really doesn't matter. i don't really understand why these definitional issues are important, since we're the ones defining the terms in the first place. but you do have a point about the lack of intimacy when typing...makes a lot of sense.

(though this is more of a general rumination, ie procrastination - obviously writing on paper seems to work best for you & i'm not trying to say your thoughts are any foggier than mine at the moment)
