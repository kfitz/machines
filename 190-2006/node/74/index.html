Sometimes I hate my life.

Submitted by racinian on 13 September 2006 - 5:19pm.
  • Janine Marchessault

At the risk of this sounding like a cop-out, I must say I have absolutely no idea how to respond to Marchessault's article, aside from complaining about my inability to understand her point. I found the beginning (meaning, the paragraph about her adventure in the bathroom) and the last section interesting, beyond that her article made me want to cry. I think, in part, this is because I am unfamiliar with a majority of the theorists she references (aside from good ol' Barthes), and in part because I can't concentrate on an article which requires me to "unpack" each sentence.

However, out of a sense of obligation, I'm going to attempt to respond to this text. Actually, I have more of a "discussion question" than a response, because everyone loves those, right?

Marchessault writes: "If there is a resistance to feminism on the part of some women, a desire not to identify with women, it is perhaps because a history, without memory, continues to divide us negatively. The task before us, then, is to fill that history not with uncertainty but with many certitudes" (88). (I realize revive responded to this section already, but I wish to take it in another direction.) At the risk of misinterpreting this point, should all women be expected to relate, or identify, each other? And is this even desirable? Like others, I'm uncomfortable with the binary inherent in this section of the text.
