What are you talking about, I totally don't listen to John Mayer

Submitted by oneoutofseven on 12 November 2006 - 3:52pm.
  • blogs
  • john mayer

If anyone has heard John Mayer's new album "Continuum" (which if you don't want admit, that's alright), you may have noticed that it is maybe a little bit, well maybe, sort of errrr... okay. And by okay, I mean okay in that same way that some Gwen Stefani songs are okay: though you feel like you should know better, somehow these songwriters are able to engage that small "audio crack" receptor in the brain, and you can't stop listening to them no matter how hard you try to go back to your usual playlists.

So yesterday, because I was so surprised at suddenly liking John Mayer again, I decided to go in search of more information--and where better to turn than www.johnmayer.com! The site, as most artist sites now are, was a jumble of text, video, images and audio clips, and interestingly enough, a blog. Obviously, I had explore.

Here's where things got interesting. Within the space of the thirty minutes of reading John Mayer's blog, I a) felt shame and fright that someone might come into the room and know I was thinking about liking John Mayer, b) thought he was a genius in his latest blog entry about the Borat movie (seriously, go read it, it's great), and c) was thoroughly charmed by the sentence: "And if you're still wondering what leg I have to stand on with this, just remember: I was truly hip for three weeks back in 2001." Uhm, amazing. I was ready to go out and sing the John Mayer Gospel all over campus.

But then I found excerpts from his first book, "The Pioneer". And then I found out he really likes Keane and says that song "Is It Any Wonder?" is "rocking his cranial jukebox". Oh no. Celebrity blog TMI.

This is an example of one of the strangest things about blogs, particularly celebrity blogs: suddenly, we're far closer to these figures than was ever before imaginable. Worse than writing e-letters to fan club members (a la Britney Spears), blogs allow artists a freedom from discretion and self-censorship that perhaps could be done without.

So now, in regards to John Mayer (I know you were wondering), I'm back to where I started. It is obviously still definitely uncool to admit that I've played his cover of "Bold as Love" 30 times since acquiring the album half a month ago, and I certainly can't share with my friends what I've been listening to lately (though I have already been thoroughly chastised by those weirdos who audio-stalk me on AudioScrobbler). Maybe that's the thing about blogs--they always confirm what you kinda sort of already maybe know, but you've just got to explore, just in case.
