It subscribes to the same theatrical methods of a full-length feature film. I think it really demonstrates how we, the consumers, have come to expect some sort of "story" with our games, and the industry has responded by attempting to link their products to the successful multi-billion dollar movie industry (even though, as we learn in the book, the game industry is larger). I feel as though I've seen dozens of flashy, bright, melodramatic trailers for video games. They are hard to take seriously, because come on! It's a video game. It's not like the user is ever going to make their character do anything but destroy bad guys.
