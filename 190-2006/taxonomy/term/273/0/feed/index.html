MS 190: Authorship - anthropological artwork http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/taxonomy/term/273/0 en Bujalskian - is that even a word? http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/262 <p>When we have discussed filmmakers and directors as authors, we've mostly talked about the role of cinematographers or writers or producers, but somehow avoided discussing the role of actors in the authorship of a film. True, they are mostly pretty, vapid, blank slates for the director to carve into his vision. Paul Walker- definitely not an author. But in some cases, they have a huge impact on the text. I was reading an old article I clipped out of the LA Times about Andrew Bujalski's new film "Mutual Appreciation." I had pulled it because of one quote about his characters, where he said that they "get reconsidered retroactively through the prism of whoever I asked to play them." We forget that actors often rewrite their characters, and certainly they at least "write" the character with their own specific mannersims and what they bring to the role.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/262">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/262#comments anthropological artwork Fri, 20 Oct 2006 16:24:46 +0000 achillesheel 262 at http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006
