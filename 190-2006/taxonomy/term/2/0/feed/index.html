While I do agree that text should be interpreted through the eyes of the reader and deconstructed for what it actually is, I do not think the author needs to be completely dismissed. Learning about an author after interpreting one of their texts can introduce a new criticism or shed new light upon the text that the reader would not have seen without that knowledge. I do believe it is important to acknowledge that there is never a single author of a work (society, surroundings, family, etc. all influence an author). Foucault talks about how literary works are a result of cultural settings, not a single individual.
