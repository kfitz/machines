Authorship and the Price is Right

Submitted by msblogger1220 on 6 November 2006 - 9:47pm.
  • awesomeness
  • random

Basically, I went on the Price is Right today and I need to blog more, so I am going to write about the experience and connect it to authorship. First of all, ridiculous people go on the Price is Right. It was amazing... people from Florida with shirts that said "My Gator is Neutered" are a prime example. Anyway, I went on the show with my rugby team (and a few friends of ruggers) and a girl on the team was picked (Angie Martinez, she goes to Pitzer).
It got me thinking... I was reminded of our discussion of Authorship and Film because I got to see so many elements of the show happening, such as the cameras and all the crew running around to make sure everything was right. It allowed me to see how many people really are involved in the process. And also it got me thinking about the formulaic narratives we have been discussing with Hamlet on the Holodeck and First Person. The Price is Right follows such a formula, it's almost ridiculous. However, the audience plays a role in the outcome of the show and plays an important part in the show environment. The contestants are picked from the audience and typically the members of the audience are frequent viewers, so the viewer is somewhat involved in the outcome of the show. And while the producers pick who those selected audience members are, the audience is still involved in creating the atmosphere and helping the game players choose prices.

Dr. Phil's "House of Hatred"

Submitted by ghostwriter on 5 November 2006 - 12:53pm.
  • random
  • TV
  • wtf

not to be confused with Mark Z. Danielewski's House of Leaves. So basically, Dr. Phil has a new show called "House of Hatred" in which 6 very different people have to live in a house together, have their lives taped, and find out what happens when people stop being polite and start getting real. Actually, that's "The Real World", but the idea is similar. But in Dr. Phil's house, the people are literally positioned as the binary opposite of another person in the house and forced to room with this person. Pair number 1 is the white supremacist guy and the black woman who hates white people, followed by the thin girl who hates fat people and the 600 lb man who hates thin people, and lastly the homophobe and the straight-hating lesbian. Everything about this premise just rubs me the wrong way. First of all, for a guy like Dr. Phil who's known for saying "Get real!", this is incredibly contrived, and the binaries are so extreme whereas in real life, issues of ignorance and/or hate are so much more complex than this. Which brings me to my second point, which is that it eschews any attempt at a more complex (and more relevant) discussion about difference in our society by reducing the housemates to representing one thing in a binary rather than as complete people. Plus the name of the show is pretty awful.
