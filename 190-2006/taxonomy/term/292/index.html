I'm reminded of a sort of funny story I heard a while ago from an unreliable source. There was this physics guy who was sick of everyone talking about philosophy all the time, and since it would take way too long to make fun of each philosophy person individually, he decided to make fun of them all at once. So he wrote a philosophy paper and submitted it to some philosophy magazine, which accepted and printed it. The fake philosophy paper swept across the nation, taking humanities departments by storm. It was, by all accounts, a roaring success, a breakthrough, a coup d'etat that upset philosophy curriculums throughout the known world, ruining the lives and reputations of hundreds of tenured philosophy professors, and the careers of thousands of promising up-and-comers. U.S. suicides saw a 3% increase per capita that year, and suicide notes, by and large, contained longer words. It was a real problematic. Then people found out it was a joke, yada yada, everyone was mad.
