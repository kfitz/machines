MS 190: Authorship - intervention http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/taxonomy/term/700/0 en Addicted to Addiction http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/593 <p>I"ve just spent the last two hours watching the A&amp;E series called Intervention - and wow it may have just become my new obsession as much as it disturbs me. I have yet to decide how I feel ethically about the show. I should explain the premise briefly, so that y'all understand the disturbing depths of the concept. They find someone who agrees to be on a show about addiction (I think that's all their told), then they have their lives taped for a few weeks or so, then they are thrust into an unexpected intervention - all under the watchful eye of the camera. You see a girl looking for tricks to turn in order to buy heroin, an ex-ceo crying in his storage unit that contains his only remaining possessions, a middle-aged alcoholic falling out of her car. It is intimate to say the least. Actually, I'm pretty sure that it is downright exploitative. At times it's almost uncomfortable to be watching these so intensely personal moments, but you can't look away at the same time.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/593">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/593#comments intervention Mon, 04 Dec 2006 07:58:19 +0000 achillesheel 593 at http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006
