MS 190: Authorship - bling and money http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/taxonomy/term/549/0 en selling out can be sneaky http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/478 <p>Simultaneously one of the funniest and more important articles that I have read in a while: Business week's "Hip Hop two-step over product placement." First off, it includes a photo of one the whiter shade of pale kind of middle-aged man who freely admits to knowing little to nothing about hip hop music, saying he's more of a "Van Morrison/Eric Clapton kind of guy." But the article includes snipets of an interview with Russell Simmons, hip hop impressario extraordinaire, who is talking about a deal with McDonalds to pay hip hop and rap artists for including things like "Big Mac" in their lyrics. but he says the deal might not work unless listeners are unaware of said agreement. In other words, Micky D's is uninterested in publicity that is not sneaky and underhanded. Simmons had the idea to instead produce songs in which rappers would rap about how mcdonald's wants to throw money at them to reposition their brand and thus create a criticism of the exploitation of hip hop culture and also corruption in adversiting. But then wasn't it simmons that brokered the deal for P. Diddy to pen "Pass the Courvosier" to reposition that brand? The whole thing seems pretty tricky to me. I may just be naive and optimistic, but I usually figure even commercial artist aren't always just plugging a product. I'm not saying creative advertising has no place, I just think it would be too bad if art was reduced to a series of commercials. As David Kiley characterizes hip hop, I just want it to return to "a lot of lyrics about bling and money."</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/478">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/478#comments all good Big Macs bling and money Fri, 17 Nov 2006 06:52:33 +0000 achillesheel 478 at http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006
