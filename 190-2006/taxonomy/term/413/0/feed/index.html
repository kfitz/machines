MS 190: Authorship - jenkins http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/taxonomy/term/413/0 en Don't Want to Grow Up http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/383 <p>Last class, we talked about Henry Jenkins's middle ground interpretation of video games: they should be viewed as neither story (narratologists' view) nor as games (ludologists' view,) but rather "as spaces ripe with narrative possibility." There is oftentimes no pre-set story or predetermined contest. The beauty of video games is in ability of the user to control so much. This perspective is very similar to that of Nick Montfort's, which analyzes interactive fiction (IF). Montfort considers many appropriate analogies for IF: story, games storygame, novel, world, literature, puzzle, problem, riddle, and machine. In the end, he believes the "world" analogy is most appropriate: "In IF, it is the world (like the literary 'setting') that is essential--characters and plot can be dispensed with, but a system is not IF unless it simulates a world, however erratically and in however limited a way" (313). Like Jenkins, Montfort asserts the importance of space in new media.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/383">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/383#comments interactive fiction jenkins montfort world Sun, 05 Nov 2006 05:00:50 +0000 WildCherry15 383 at http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006
