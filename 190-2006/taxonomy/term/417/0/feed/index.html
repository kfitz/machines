In one of my other classes, we have been studying the perceptual changes in people that accompanied the Industrial Age, epitomized in the railroad. That is when society's need for speed really took off. That is when time and space began to dramatically shrink. There wasn't yet a global village, but the idea of a nation began. For example, it was because of the Industrial Age that many scattered parts of Germany coalesced into a nation in the late 1800s.
