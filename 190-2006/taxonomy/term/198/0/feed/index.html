Granted I am a bit of a wuss when it comes to anything remotely frightening. I.E. I can't even handle watching the previews for The Grudge 2. But-- You would think I could read this book at night, honestly. Last night I tried really tried and I was so fully imagining the pressence in my dark room that I had to put the thing down. But, I mean it isn't even really scary, creepy yes, psychologically challenging, but not really that scary hmmm. </p> <p>I just think it is interested that I feel like I have been so influenced by the tone of the book as set by "Johnny". If it weren't for being told at the beginning of the book that I was going to be in for a mind tripping ride I don't think I would find the tone so daunting. Really the naritive of the Navison Story isn't that unsettling. A little strange, but if I didn't have Johnny intermitidly telling me how life altering it was I don't think I would be frightened.
