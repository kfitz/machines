What follows is Ulrich's statement, read to the U.S. Senate in 2000. I've obnoxiously copy-and-pasted the whole thing into this blog, but if you want to read it from the site, go <a href="http://www.encycmet.com/news/2000-07-12c.shtml">HERE</a>. Ulrich's statement is honest and heartfelt, but just a tad annoying because 1) Lessig and Vaidy both mentioned that there is no PROOF that downloading ever hurts the artists or even the industry and 2) If anything, downloading hurts the actual musician the LEAST out of everyone else (the distribution company, manager, etc.). They get a tiny fraction of the overall yield of their record sales; they make much more money off personal appearances and concerts.
