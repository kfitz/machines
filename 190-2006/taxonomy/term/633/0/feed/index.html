MS 190: Authorship - post-postmodernism http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/taxonomy/term/633/0 en Post-Postmodernism: "Age of Synthesis" http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/544 <p>So I've been kind of fascinated with the idea of post-postmodernism lately. I used to just blow it off, because the name was too meta for my taste. But it is actually a cool concept: a return to the unity fractured by postmodernism. The best, most succinct definition I found was "Age of Synthesis"<a href="http://www.gardenvisit.com/landscape/architecture/1.7-post-postmodernism.htm ">[source 1]</a>.</p> <p>This is different than flat-out modernism (which also claims to unify), because it is more aware of itself as a construction, "aware of its own failures, insubstantiality, and secondariness" <a href="http://www.focusing.org/apm_papers/epstein.html">[source 2]</a>. Also different from the modern industrial notion of unity, the "Age of Synthesis" unity is more natural, traditional, and "lyrical"--a reaction to technological disillusionment.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/544">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/544#comments feminism internet post-postmodernism Wed, 29 Nov 2006 07:24:03 +0000 WildCherry15 544 at http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006
