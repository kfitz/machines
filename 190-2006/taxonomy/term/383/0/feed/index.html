MS 190: Authorship - mind mapping http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/taxonomy/term/383/0 en Mind Maps http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/347 <p>Instead of annotated bibliographies, those of us doing new media projects are turning in "drafted project outlines elaborating on the project's structure". Professor Fitzpatrick suggested that for my project, I use mind mapping software to lay out the structure of my thesis, which was pretty much suggesting that I reunite after a year with one of my favorite programs on Earth: Free Mind.</p> <p>Wikipedia defines a mind map as "an image-centered diagram that represents semantic or other connections between portions of information. By presenting these connections in a radial, non-linear graphical manner, it encourages a brainstorming approach to any given organizational task, eliminating the hurdle of initially establishing an intrinsically appropriate or relevant conceptual framework to work within". Basically, picture the brainstorming with little bubbles that you did on paper in elementary school, but far more sophisticated, easily editable, and on the computer.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/347">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/347#comments mind mapping thesis Tue, 31 Oct 2006 20:59:39 +0000 oneoutofseven 347 at http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006
