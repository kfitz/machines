MS 190: Authorship - nostalgia http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/taxonomy/term/352/0 en Duck Amuck http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/323 <p>I hope everyone liked the presentation our group gave for Hamlet on the Holodeck. I had a lot of fun--and not just with the wine. I thought it was cool that we could integrate so much real-life stuff into the theoretical dissections of literary development. I am thinking, in particular, of "Duck Amuck"--the Daffy Duck cartoon.</p> <p>This cartoon was fun to watch because I felt like I was a kid again. That's involuntary, associative memory for you. In another class, I just had to read all about Proust's feelings on the unique power of involuntary memory. For him, it was a cookie, not a cartoon, that brought back childhood memories that he couldn't make himself remember. At any rate, there is power in the object that can do that: awaken emotions and visceral memories that the intellect and just words can't reach.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/323">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/323#comments duck amuck hamlet on the holodeck janet murray nostalgia Sat, 28 Oct 2006 12:28:25 +0000 WildCherry15 323 at http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006
