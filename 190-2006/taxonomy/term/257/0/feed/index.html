You guys would make a lovely couple http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/581 <p>While visiting my usual online haunts I found a blog to blog about! Virginia Heffernan's "Screens" blog discovered a new use of YouTube! And to think we had exhausted YouTube's potential--</p> <p>Heffernan reports on a YouTuber who found a way to make money: homemade advertisements for big corporations! The goal was to finance two months rent in San Francisco by scamming The Man. The unnamed YouTuber picked up a vintage Nike t-shirt, gave it to a friend to wear at a David Rodigan concert (D.J. RamJam), and film the whole thing. Next, he sent a letter to Nike explaining the project, and when the video received 15,000 hits, the user received a check for $1,815.88 from Nike. As Heffernan points out, the video didn't attract viewers because it was an advertisement for Nike (at least not initially), it attracted viewers because of the DJ.
