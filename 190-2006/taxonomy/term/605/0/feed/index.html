MS 190: Authorship - Diogenes http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/taxonomy/term/605/0 en Masturbating in the Marketplace http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/519 <p>In chapter 3, Vaidhyanathan (I should probably learn how to pronounce that) references two different types of cynicism. The first pertains to Diogenes (ancient Greek who would whack it in the marketplace) and is referred to as "Diogenic cynicism". According to Vaidy: "Diogenic cynicism values discipline, self-sufficiency, and rejecting the influence of social convention or cultural power" (26). The other type of cynicism is "Costanzan", referencing George Costanza of SIENFELD. Costanzan cynicism is "disengaged" and "selfish" (26). The overall impression is that Diogenic cynicism is socially responsible, while Constanzan cynicism is egotistical.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/519">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/519#comments cynicism Diogenes Mon, 27 Nov 2006 07:15:44 +0000 DaLynziiChic 519 at http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006
