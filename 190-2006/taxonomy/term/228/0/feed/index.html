MS 190: Authorship - naming http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/taxonomy/term/228/0 en What if I changed my name? http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/214 <p>So, not entirely related to anything, but something interesting and hopefully preceeding more relevant material...<br /> As some of you may or may not know, the band I'm in recently changed our name. Someone asked me at Symposium one night if I thought the new name made any difference. I immediately said it did. I felt freed as an author. The old name had come to symbolize a certain set of musical requirements, and the new one to me represents creative freedom. It symbolizes a willingness, almost a necessity, to step outside my personal boundaries of familiarity, and still somehow manages to encapsulate a more cohesive end product. My creative process has been restructured at every level by simply assigning a new name to the collective author of the final product. I'm not sure why this is, how it relates to being one author in a group of four - or seeing so many changes undergo the creative product from start to finish - or whether the old name had become an author-function representing a body of work I had outgrown...</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/214">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/214#comments authorship awesomeness music naming Wed, 11 Oct 2006 07:45:10 +0000 BuildingsAndFood 214 at http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006
