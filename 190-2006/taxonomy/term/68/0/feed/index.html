MS 190: Authorship - banksy http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/taxonomy/term/68/0 en Brangelina loves Banksy... http://machines.pomona.edu.190-2006/node/140 <p>and so do a lot of other celebs who attended the "Barely Legal" show (Keanu, Jude Law, McCauley Culkin, Xtina).<br /> Unfortunately I didn't make it to the show so I can't comment directly, but evidently and according to LA weekly writer Linda Immediato, the audience took an ironic turn for the worse. In addition to the high star power, there were a bunch of "well-dressed sorts in expensive clothing... a handful of miscreant graffiti kids, a few tattoed rabble rousers, but by and large the crowd was college-educated gorgeous. Everywhere I turned, my ears were flooded with people praising the show: 'brilliant,' 'amazing,' 'genius,' blah blah blah, 'important.'" She thought she was in the wrong place. The scene she depicts makes my stomach turn even in print. And I know it's lame to let art be affected by the other people who like it, but I (and Linda, evidently) am affected. the simple fact that some idiot likes the same thing I might enjoy makes me have disdain for it. anyway, back to banksy. what's interesting to me is that (assuming Linda wasn't the only one with this response) is that the composition of the audience affected the actual art... It seems that Banksy's intent (whatever revolutionary social change it might be) was overshadowed or even subverted by the overwhelming prescence and acceptance of those "college-educated gorgeous." In the end, Immediato makes the genius comparison between the exhibition's reception and "the Banksy sketch of people lined up to buy T-shirts that read 'destroy capitalism.'" So maybe this is the kind of hypocritical scene Banksy craves - mainstream richies hobnobbing and talking about how "brilliant" and "important" doctoring up Paris Hilton CDs was, and all the while gawking at Christina Aguilera.
