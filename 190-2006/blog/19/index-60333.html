Narcissism

  • gummybeary juice
  • krauss
  • manovich

In the Manovich reading, I thought the comment of video/computers as narcissistic to be very interesting.

Now days it seems like everything is made to be personalized in some aspect or another: color, shape, size, sound. We buy ringtones, we get the pink RAZR, we put up picture wallpaper, we get those stupid jinglejangles and skins...and that's just for our cell phones! Computers are almost worse.

Let's take for instance, my computer, a.k.a. POOTER and s.k.a. OKCOMPUTER. The fact that I have such names is the first hint of its narcissistic power; of its ability to reflect an image of me within its content. On my background is a picture of my horse, Frank. Almost everyone has some sort of personalized wallpaper for their computer and almost always it is a picture of themselves. Sometimes it is an image from Digital Blasphemy, which are usually pretty neat. But I digress.

Game Trailers

Submitted by DaLynziiChic on 1 November 2006 - 2:52am.
  • blah

One thing that I thought was interesting in the readings was Zimmerman's reference to video games suffering from a bad case of "cinema envy".

I think this is pretty evident in this trailer.

Granted, this video game is being released with the MOVIE Eragon, but still. It subscribes to the same theatrical methods of a full-length feature film. I think it really demonstrates how we, the consumers, have come to expect some sort of "story" with our games, and the industry has responded by attempting to link their products to the successful multi-billion dollar movie industry (even though, as we learn in the book, the game industry is larger). I feel as though I've seen dozens of flashy, bright, melodramatic trailers for video games. They are hard to take seriously, because come on! It's a video game. It's not like the user is ever going to make their character do anything but destroy bad guys. And yet the marketing strategy is successful.

DOAP

Submitted by DaLynziiChic on 31 October 2006 - 1:53am.
  • Death of a President
  • sugartits

Something other than video games:

I'm sure a lot of you have heard about the film DEATH OF A PRESIDENT, a faux-doc on the assassination of George W. Bush. It's been destroyed by some critics and hailed by others, and won the International Critics award at the Toronto Fest.

Its distributor, Newmarket, is having one hell of a time distributing the film in American markets. Theaters are blackballing them like no other. AMC claims "We don't feel it's appropriate to play a film that depicts the assassination of our current president." (Entertainment Weekly, #905, pg 18)

Interestingly enough, Newmarket is responsible for the release of everyone's favorite antisemetic take on HOSTEL...none other than Gibson's THE PASSION OF THE CHRIST. Execs seem comfortable with the limited release, knowing that being blacklisted can greatly aid a film's popularity. People will certainly hear about the film, and if they want to see it, they'll see it.

I need to brush my teeth.

Submitted by DaLynziiChic on 26 October 2006 - 2:07pm.
  • tv shows and the internet

One of the things that was brought up last night in class was the relationship between television and online content. I believe Murray was hoping for a relationship where viewers can directly affect and change content. Although this is apparent in shows such as AMERICAN IDOL, I really don't see it reaching beyond that. However, shows DO listen to input from their fans in order to better accomodate and attract their fan base. For instance, XENA Warrior Princess developed a large following of lesbian viewers who read deeply into the "subtext" between Gabrielle and Xena. Once producers realized this, they tended to play out this subtext more and more, subtly giving viewers more to work with. The last two seasons of XENA are COMPLETELY different from the first two due to this change in target audience.
