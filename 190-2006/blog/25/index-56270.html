New Thesis/Project focus.....

Submitted by reliablesrc07 on 1 November 2006 - 1:50pm.
  • thesis-identity-SNS-MySpace-Facebook

My original project/thesis proposal had decidedly been to broad to be able to pursue in a way that allow for me to really explore effectively. So I researched the topic regarding identity and social networking systems and I concluded that the most pertinent SNS to people my age wre MysSpace and Facebook. And I realized that both were completely different in terms of who joined and how they portrayed themselves. And then Facebook opened up to the public in an attempt to become more inclusive. And Myspace has recenetly allowed for profiles to be blocked to the public. As has facebook. So myspace is becoming more exclusive and facebook more inclusive. Is this an attempt by both to attract both people who lobe being in the public eye and people who desire a greater level of privacy? How does the interface affect the overall usage? And this is how I came to find that what really really really interests me in terms of SNS is how the differences in the type of network design and interface affect the network identities that myspace and facebook users present and how these identities in turn affect the types of connections the users' experience with each other. Do you guys think that this question is something that the average type of user asks themselves when they log on? Do you think they make a conscious effort to project a certain identity or do you think that the site design inherently affects the type of identity presented and thus the type and extent of connections made with other users?

Vertov's A man with a movie camera

Submitted by reliablesrc07 on 10 October 2006 - 8:52pm.
  • vertov-movie camera-authorship-camera speed

So today (10/09) we watched Man with A movie Camera in another class of mine.

Watching it a second time after having our class discussion really allowed for me to focus more in depth in what the movie was about.

Anyhow, before the movie began, my film professor mentioned that the soundtrack on the movie (the same one we listened to in our video in class) is indeed the original one that Vertov himself comisisoned to go with his movie. He wanted the music to accompany the pace of his movie. This was Vertov;s way of creating a musical ballet mechanique, or musical montage of the moving elements in his film. After watching it a second time, I was convinced that the sound in this film is of equal or greater value than the visuals because a great sound can make up for a mediocre visual. Not to mention that the way that the music was juxtaposed to the filmic action really did affect how I perceived the film. It really did, along with the changing speed of the film, make my emotions come and go in waves. Watching this film in silence would be like watching the stabbing scene in Hitcock's Psycho minus the music--. The result would be "eh--" Music and film speed by create a unique rhythm together that Vertoov as author really took advantage of.

Project Proposal block

Submitted by reliablesrc07 on 8 October 2006 - 8:41pm.
  • identity management-social networking services-social networks

After some research, I pretty much have a general idea of where I want to go with the final project paper. I'm just not sure where to take it exactly.... This is what I have written down concretely so far:

"According to Wikipedia, a social network is understood to mean "a social structure made of nodes which are generally individuals or organizations. It indicates the ways in which they are connected through various social familiarities ranging from casual acquaintance to close familial bonds." With the introduction of internet based social networks, formally known as a social networking services, or SNS, users are allowed to build social networks through a social software and connect to other users based on whatever interests they desire. An internet based social network allows its users to create an identity that may either be acurate or completely inaccurate depending on what they want to portray. Additionally, the overall accessibility to a social networking service is high, therefore allowing almost anyone to join, which may or may not pose a problem in terms of privacy. What this paper will attempt to do is explore and gain a better understanding of the impact that social networking services are having on identity management issues."
