Project Proposal: Google Earth

By SariKayla - Posted on 21 March 2007 - 4:16pm.
Tagged:
  • project proposal

I intend to focus my critical project on the technology of "Google Earth." For those who are unfamiliar with this, Google Earth is one of the more recently developed aspects of Google, where a search engine of maps, satellite imagery, and three-dimensional buildings and geographic locations can be present. As the site's homepage acknowledges, you can: "Fly to your house. Just type in an address, press search, and you'll zoom right in. Search for schools, parks, restaurants, and hotels. Get driving directions, tilt and rotate the view to see 3D terrain and buildings."

I propose to research and provide a detailed analysis of the functions that Google Earth offers, in addition to the companies and people in particular that benefit from the site. I hope to discover the positive and negative elements to this form of technology, as well as the people and places most likely affected. Above all, I will question the existence of Google Earth â€“ how our society as a whole may or may not benefit from this form of technology, and whether the program is an invasion of privacy for many individuals.

I will also relate the Google Earth technology to selections from recent class readings, addressing topics seen in "The Coming of Age of the Flesh Machine," which notes the "danger posed as technology increasingly influences politics, science, and attitudes toward the body." And in the case of Google Earth, technology surpasses these more developed aspects of our lives and exposes even our most fundamental information: our physical location in the world.

great focus
Submitted by KF on 1 April 2007 - 12:27pm.

I'm really interested in your focus on Google Earth, and will look forward to seeing what your project uncovers. You need to make sure that you're not just offering a description of the site and its functionality, though; you need to develop an analytical, interpretive research question that will guide your project, one that's a bit more specific than whether this technology is beneficial or not. For instance, you might focus on the issue of surveillance: what kinds of dangers does the ready availability of satellite imagery present to personal freedom? Or you might think about our relationship to place -- how does our sense of "home" change when we can get such an instantaneous picture of the relationship between the local and the global? And so on -- you should focus on the question that most interests you, but you need to make sure that it's one key question, and that it's really specific, in order to make your analysis go as deep as you can. I'll look forward to seeing where you go with this.
