Our culture and technology

By SariKayla - Posted on 1 March 2007 - 12:31pm.
Tagged:
  • class discussion

After class yesterday, as I came back to my room and sat down to work on a paper, I really started to think about how lucky we are. While Professor Fitzpatrick wrote her college papers on a typewriter, we have the power to use Microsoft Word â€“ with the options of spell-check, a thesaurus, and a number of different fonts and other useful tools. And before all of this, papers were hand-written. Research was done in the library through a supply of books and newspaper articles. Nobody had the power to type his or her essay while simultaneously researching information offered on the Internet.
I guess what really came to my attention is the convenience provided in our technological advancements. I started to wonder whether we take for granted what we have. Our society seems to be at a place where we do not necessarily appreciate how easy we have it. All we want is the next hottest form of technology out on the market, and once we get a hold of it, we lose sight of what life was like before we could take advantage of its useful functions. In fact, we get to a place where we could not imagine living life without it. With this concept, I am primarily thinking along the lines of computers, cellular phones, and as Williams discussed in his essay, the television.
Does anyone else ever sit back and wonder how different life would be if we were not offered some of the media developments that are out there today?
