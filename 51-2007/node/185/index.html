the sin of computer illiteracy

By ColoradoGirl - Posted on 27 March 2007 - 2:51pm.
Tagged:
  • technological divide

In her essay "Who am We?" Sherry Turkle writes about how "real life may itself be just one more window" (on a computer screen). She also tells the story of Tim, who even though he doesn't know what an orgot is can still play SimLife. I found this rant of an essay, and was curious about how computer illiteracy is being approached today?? How are people who don't know computers inside and out being excluded from new social structures? Should we even care? Where can the study of New Media take us on this dilemma of pro-technological development, yet social responsibility?

* I googled the author of the essay, Prof. Allen Downey--he teaches at Olin College of Engineering, if you care.

People do what is necessary
Submitted by Bumpkins on 27 March 2007 - 3:09pm.

I think those of us who do not know every in and out of computers get others to do it for us. I know my grandparents are not afraid to call if they can't figure something out on their computer, and my roommate tutors local old ladies on how to use computers. They will never get into facebook or myspace or even blogging, but if they really wanted to they could. People can live fine outside of the digital / computer world, e.g. everyone before 1990. We may not understand how people lived without technology, but apparently they did. I think our only obligation, if it can be called that, is to give everyone the opportunity to use / learn computers if they so desire.

Submitted by ColoradoGirl on 27 March 2007 - 3:28pm.

I totally agree with you, and I'm not trying to play the devil's advocate . . . but HOW can we give everyone the opportunity to use/ learn computers?? In this New Media course at a very exclusive private college, I still feel left behind. (Ahhh!)

I feel like our education system needs a re-configuring (so as to address Nelson's "Computer Lib . . .") so students can interact with technology directly. HOW can we do this in our current socio-political set-up?? This is one of the biggest dilemmas of comtemporary life I see, and especially in regard to new media. Let's save the world!!
