Introduction to New Media - collective authorship http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net.51-2007/taxonomy/term/33/0 en recovering collectivity http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net.51-2007/node/40 <p>I just read an article about authorship and the individual genius for another class -- <a href="http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/IPCoop/92wood.html">Martha Woodmansee's On the Author Effect: Recovering Collectivity.</a> Woodmansee asks right off the bat: "Will the author in the modern sense prove to have been only a brief episode in the history of writing?" She concludes it will. </p> <p>Had Woodmansee been able to forsee Wiki technology (she wrote the article in 1991) I think she would have made her point MUCH more vigorously.</p> <p>It seems that we still need the guarantee of original authorship on so many things (a novel, an analytic essay, etc.) but I feel the Wiki are rapidly catapulting us back into collectivity. Just looking at the history of edits on a wikipedia page (like the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=United_States&amp;action=history">United States</a> entry, for example) we see that collectivity reigns.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net.51-2007/node/40">read more</a></p> http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net.51-2007/node/40#comments collective authorship wiki Wed, 31 Jan 2007 05:27:34 +0000 ColoradoGirl 40 at http://machines.plannedobsolescence.net.51-2007
