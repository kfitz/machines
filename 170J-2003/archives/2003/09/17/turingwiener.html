Does spelling count?

Our class discussion today made me remember an incident where a professor of English at Pitzer College once complained that her students were members of the “spell check generation,” as she handed back handwritten journal entries. She went on to explain that because of advances in technology students were loosing the ability to spell correctly without the assistance of a computer.

As a kid I remember dreading taking spelling tests. The hardest words to spell were words that were not spelled like they sounded, words like phone, and cologne. Now, if I type the words exactly as they sound fone and coelone the computer tells me that I have misspelled them and correctly guesses the word I intended.

The initial emphasis on correct spelling was to establish a universal method of communication (universal in that all people of a particular language can comprehend the idea expressed) so that people could communicate effectively in a written form. Do children still need to be taught how to spell since a computer spell checker can perform this task for us?

What if spell check eliminates spelling tests from elementary school curriculum or if proper spelling is not required on a computer spelling test as long as one can guess the intended word? Is this a big deal or does spelling really matter after all since we are headed into a paperless environment? I must say I am rather jealous if the traditional spelling tests taken by school age children are deleted or changed at some point due to these advances in technology. I cannot help but wonder if spell check is detrimental to society or an advancement and an aid to society?
