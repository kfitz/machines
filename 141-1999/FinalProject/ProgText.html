"Within a few years Macondo was a village that was more orderly and hard-working than any known until then by its three hundred inhabitants. It was truly a happy village where no one was over thirty years of age and where no one had died.... That spirit of social initiative disappeared in a short time, pulled away by the fever of the magnets, the astronomical calculations, the dreams of transmutation, and the urge to discover the wonders of the world. From a clean and active man, Jose Arcadio Buendia changed into a lazy man in appearance, careless in his dress, with a wild beard that Ursula managed to trim with great effort and a kitchen knife (9-10)."
