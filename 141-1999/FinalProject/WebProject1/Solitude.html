--Úrsula's Attempt to Break the Solitude--

Úrsula's attack against Macondo's devastation inevitably leads her to the room where José Arcadio Buendía remains isolated. This isolation represents the root of her familily's despair. This scene reveals Úrsula's understanding that time does not "pass", but instead continually repeats itself. Her efforts to end José Arcadio Buendía 's solitude demonstrate that ending the isolation is the only method by which the circle of devastation which inflicts Macondo can be interupted. If Márquez's text is a commentary upon Latin America the sentiment is clear. Latin America's self-imposed solitude from the outside world causes a vicious cycle of poverty and exploitation. Úrsula represents an enlightened leader who understands that the solitude must end in order to gain genuine prosperity.
