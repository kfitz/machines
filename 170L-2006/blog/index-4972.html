Submitted by marmalade on 26 November 2006 - 5:20pm.
  • Hayles

I was reading the Hayles at like 4:30 in the morning in an over-crowded, really ugly airport, so I'm not sure that any of my thoughts on her paper will make much sense, but bear with me!

I have this feeling that Hayles is trying to change the way we view the world altogether in this piece--on every page there are big claims that ring a bit like McLuhan's "the medium is the message." (Actually, doesn't her discussion on 73 about how the literatry corpus is similar to the human body, in that it "is at once a physical object and a space of representation, a body and a message", smack of McLuhan?)

Who are we?

I pedalled home through Little Shanghai today and stopped at the market that steams of fish and leeks. I always peddle through the wet street outside of it. They hose it all down. Today I got down and walked with everyone over the wet concrete past the news stands and between the aisles to smell the ageing vegetables, the pickles, and the spice.

If I lived on the field I worked and flooded it, if I ate the produce of that field and drank the irrigation, how much difference would remain between the swamps outside and inside of me?
