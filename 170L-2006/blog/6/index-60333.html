Unintended Materiality

Submitted by crashingintowalls on 11 October 2006 - 9:56am.
  • Materiality
  • Memmott

I was interested by silversprung's question about whether materiality is that much of a big deal for authors not aware of their own materiality. Clearly, there are authors like Mark Danielewski who take great pride in proclaiming and commenting on their materiality, but it does seem that this is a given for the vast majority of authors.

Reading "Lexia to Perplexia," I (here I was about to say enjoyed, but that isn't quite the right coloration) was provoked by the issue of the "face" that we impose onto a computer monitor. Among other things, it brought to mind the biases that we bring to reading a text, in this case a mapping from our human experience of face-to-face conversation.

Power dynamics of hypertext

Submitted by crashingintowalls on 27 September 2006 - 12:54pm.
  • hypertext fiction

Apologies that this doesn't depart from somewhere more novel, but I'd like to pick at one of the areas that is drawing in many of us, including silversprung and marmalade. So, who is in "control" in a hypertext, anyway? Is it the "author," insofar as she defines and thus limits the possiblities for exploration? Is it the "wreader" who chooses paths and continues or discontinues participation? Our class discussion got at a prevalant feeling that "Afternoon" was something different from a choose your own adventure story, but we never got all the way to qualifying the differences or similarities.

Not all who wander are lost

Submitted by crashingintowalls on 6 September 2006 - 9:13am.
  • searching

I came across this rather common quote three years ago at a gas station off of Highway 101. A group of high school friends and I were driving out to go on a backpacking trip to celebrate the end of our senior year. We stopped at a gas station near the trailhead to fill up the vehicles, and as I got out to stretch I noticed this singular rusted-out VW Beetle parked (well, perhaps I assumed that the situation was less intentional and more permanent than "parked") next to the garage. Blazoned on its bumper was the above saying, and it is one that has stuck with me.

The feeling of wandering was one that I had to overcome several times while on my most recent journey through the wide world of blogs. I've spent some time on personal blogs, and I definitely appreciated Gunslinger1818's comments on Facebook and marmalade's on Postsecret. Unfortunately, this took some of the wind out of my sails and I decided to search for some new blogs.
