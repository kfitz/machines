EPIC 2015, Privacy and Consent

Submitted by Lulu on 29 October 2006 - 12:48am.
  • check this out!
  • flash video recommendations
  • media history
  • the internet

Something that Jill Walker mentions in her essay about Online Caroline reminded me of a flash video that I'd seen recently. I briefly touched on the Big Brother issue at the end of my last post, but then I remembered this really relevant video.

Walker talks briefly about the Big Brother aspect of sites like Online Caroline, and then she alludes to the similar "adjustment to user's behavior [that] is used a lot in marketing." (304) For example, "Epinions.com arranges articles so the ones I see first are ones similar to others I've liked, or are highly rated by people whose writing I've rated highly." (304) And, as we all know, Amazon.com does the same thing, which I find extremely useful, except for the fact that the database is managed and processed by a computer rather than a human, so every now and then my amazon.com will think that I absolutely love emotology books about Latin roots and send me millions of such recs just because I ordered that one emotology book five years ago when I was studying for the SAT Verbal section. I'm sure we've all run into this problem before, and it reminds us that indeed computers are still just computers and not humans.

Epic 2014 is a very well-made flash video about a possible future of technology and the internet. It's basically a chronicle of the digital world and media history starting from 1989 all the way until the present and into the future. Once the video reaches 2005, it goes into a fictionalized story, presenting it as fact. Among the fictionalized "facts" that the video presents is the Google-Amazon merger, aka Googlezon. Google supplies its state-of-the-art search technology while Amazon supplies its social recommendations and commercial infrastructure, and together they provide TOTAL customization of content and advertising, using each user's demographics, reading habits, personal info, etc. That's amazing and ridiculously creepy to me. Another technology is EPIC, standing for Evolving Personalized Information Construct, a technology that Googlezon unleashes in 2014, which stores information and everything about users including consumption habits, demographics, political beliefs, etc.

This video's futuristic tone and the realism of its illustrations and art make these ideas pretty believable. I think it sounds more Big-Brotherish when it's actually stated than if/when it becomes reality. The very idea of such infiltration will result in us screaming invasion of privacy and all sorts of other accusations, but when invasive measures actually do occur, we're not always aware. In fact, we actually give our consent. I imagine that if we lived in the 1970s and someone told us that one day there would be this thing called the internet that links people in seconds or amazon.com which gives you personalized recommendations based on previous choice, we would feel uneasy and our privacy infringed upon. Now, however, we consent to such invasion because it makes our lives easier and more convenient. We willingly give up our credit card numbers, social security numbers, bank account info, and practically store all our other private information onto the internet, trusting it as secure and safe. Seems pretty creepy if this were a decade ago, right? I think so. I feel like the creepiness that I feel now in regards to the technology in EPIC 2015 will one day turn into acceptance because we will have become so used to such invasion that such technology will only seem routine and necessary in order for our lives to function.
