Hiss Boo

Submitted by black lace on 19 October 2006 - 8:32pm.
  • Adventure
  • games
  • help?
  • homework

Alright, so I'm trying to play Adventure. Rather enjoying this next week's assignments, great procrastinations. In theory, that is. In practice, this is utterly infuriating. I am not a gamer in the slightest. I know very little about any computer games, save solitaire and 8+ year old "educational" games, except they usually look kinda pretty, and are apparently addictive. The extent of my knowledge of video games is being jiggly puff while my friends play smash brothers and i stand on edges, watching my little guy wave his arms around trying to maintain balance while everyone else beats the blazes out of one another (the one time I won this way, they wouldn't let me play again all night).

I don't know how this whole game thing is meant to work. And quite frankly it's really ticking me off. It doesn't make sense! Help?

Case and Point:

>turn around
You can't see any such thing.

>turn
What do you want to turn?

>turn me
That would be less than courteous.

>walk around building
You can't see any such thing.

>look

At End Of Road
You are standing at the end of a road before a small brick building. Around you is a forest. A small stream flows out of the building and down a gully.

>walk to building
You can't see any such thing.

>walk to small brick building
You can't see any such thing.

>Walk to small brick building.
You can't see any such thing.

Amusing, certainly, espcially that bit about what would be courteous, but absolutely infuriating. If it's telling me I see it, how can I suddenly not see it without having moved?!?! What am I missing?

I realize that games have evolved rather beyond the text-based, the copyrights on these are more than 10 years old, but...it seems like a whole lot of this sort of thing is dictated by the whims of the programmers. I mean, I realize there are paramaters to any form of media, and I quite pleasantly move about in my daily life within the boundries of several. I suppose what's really frustrating me right now is the illusion of control, and the utter inability to actually weild it.

And yes, I'm the sort of person who would have played that Star Wars game exclusively for the dance parties.
