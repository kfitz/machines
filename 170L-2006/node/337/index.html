Mole's Log 04 10 2007

Submitted by magoo on 1 November 2006 - 5:28pm.
  • blog fiction
  • final project

moleslog04072007

Edna told me about the four verbs last week. I've been here two months. Couldn't someone somewhere in all that training, have said, "Look, asshole, there are only four verbs"?

"Don't worry," she said, "This sort of work is hard for people like you."

Who are people like me?

Is it the bicycle I lock to a phone pole outside the office lot? The stupid whiskers? I barely talk at LISG. I try not to look long at anyone or swing my shoulders as I walk.

"No, really," she went on, "What is it you do?"

Eat, sleep, move when poked. Until five seconds ago I smuggled documents out of the information service of a nuclear energy facility.

"I mean, do you paint, do you write?"

I'd flattered myself I'd been getting by fairly well. Edna is the hugely fat, middle-aged woman who sits beside me or across the table every morning and jokes with the dozen or so people around me who survived last week's massive layoffs. I hadn't taken her to be supernaturally perceptive, which may mean I'd noticed less than she had.

"You're not invisible, you know."

"No." How many years did I try to protect my individuality?

"I don't mean you're not getting by, but the regularity of this has to bother you, this business of having to classify things just the way someone else would, of having the supervisors review everything and send things back, when you would normally classify everything so differently."

"Yes."

She said some more to smooth my nerves, but nothing that showed she knew a thing of what made me nervous.

Then "People know things about you that they don't say, Jack, just like you know things about them. Sometimes people find it easier to not speak. Don't we?"

I decided I might need to write you.
