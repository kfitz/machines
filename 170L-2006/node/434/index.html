Creating Identity on Social Sites

Submitted by tophat1 on 14 November 2006 - 11:57pm.
  • reading outside class

I'm reading a collection of essays right now called "Communities in Cyberspace" and in it is an essay by Judith Donath called "Identity and Deception in the Virtual Community." In it she addresses one of the issues we discussed in class. We wondered why people would choose to contribute to a wiki and we came up with a few possibilities. Donath suggests that some people say altruism accounts for some contributions but she believes it is more likely that people want to assert themselves as knowledgable sources. She says that "building reputation and establishing one's online identity provides a great deal of motivation" because no matter how intelligent your post is, you can only get recognition if the author is known.

Of course, there is a flip side that she mentions which is the responsibility we have, or at times should feel, for what we do and write on these group sites. For one thing, even if you have constructed an online identity you, the person in front of the screen, do have some responsibility for your actions. For one thing, we already know that certain groups, such as pedophiles, who get online and misbehave are punished when caught. To bring the discussion back to group sites that involve people asking for and doling out information, even if you are anonymous, as Donath points out, if you give out bad information, the results can be pretty bad. For example, say someone is on a site about diabetes and you suggest taking something that could lead their condition to significantly worsen.

So, depending on the site, anonymity is not desirable and responsibility is not taken as seriously as it ought to be. It seems that, on the internet, because it is not a "real" space, we forget that our actions have consequences for other real people sitting in front of their respective screens. Ultimately, it seems like the move to assert ourselves as authors, rather than anonymous contributors, is a little more complicated.
