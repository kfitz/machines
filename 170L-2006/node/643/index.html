Last Hurrahs and all that Jazz

Submitted by grumpymutt on 15 December 2006 - 1:56am.
  • rant

At a loss for words...

Currently reading up on Alternative Dance Competitions while MC Frontalot blares via Winamp in the background (more on the genre "Nerdcore HipHop" in... umm... never)... and i'm realizing the internets [sic] are a wonderful thing. A Slashdot debate pointed me in the direction of a Business Week article about a lawsuit that (from TFA) "is reviving debate over whether Web overuse may be classified as an addiction." Not going to touch that one with a ten foot pole, but it goes something along the lines of "Sure I was chatting in a sex chat room at work but... I've got a serious addiction to the interweb so you're not allowed to fire me."

I'm not exactly sure where I was the day Al Gore invented the series of tubes that shuttle essential (mostly pr0n and Chuck Norris facts) information to the masses, but surely a greater thing has not been crafted since the first Cave-Person took it upon themselves to finally slice their bread, as opposed to beating their spouse with the solid loaf until it broke apart into palatable crumbs (ah, how i yearn for simpler times). Perhaps the wheel and office supplies (binder clips... Nice) are contenders in regards to the bread bit, but where would we be without the World Wide Web? Friend, Mentor, Provider... I could page through Dinosaur Blogs for hours upon end, but by simply sliding my mouse over to the right side of my screen I've just learned that... hrmm. A reclusive man's body was found in his home being devoured by rats, Princess Diana's car crash (nine years ago) was probably an accident, though it's still under investigation (still no cure for cancer), and... a new Stargate series is in the works. I could probably do better if I poked around a bit...

And that's the best part about the internet, Exploration. The internet brought me Darth Tater, a rather prominent conversation piece on my desk. Barring whatever elitist pundits might think, Wikipedia is perhaps the greatest font of knowledge since the Magic Eight Ball. Amazon trumps going to the Mall hands down, as you only have to deal with other people if you feel so inclined. And shopping online is better for the environment too; i'm sure you'd stumble upon plenty of studies supporting that statement if you punch in the right query on Google. BitTorrent means I don't actually need to own a television to catch up on the latest fads, not to mention the fact that I don't even have to throw away ten bucks to watch the latest Hollywood (banal corrosive insultingly-uninspired) Blockbuster. I haven't bought a CD in years, and it isn't simply because i'm a lazy, perpetually broke college student. Well... i suppose that about sums it up... which brings me to the age old debate over Pirates versus Ninjas... only the internet could've introduced Zombies and Robots and Monkeys to the mix, paralyzing the minds countless of the more esteemed philosophers for decades to come (the answer's Robots by the way, they can just assimilate the rest and don't need to eat people).

Seriously though; It's like a great big club and everyone's invited. Actually, it's more like a Greyhound Station (i've spent time in many): a rather dynamic slice of the American Pie. Boing-Boing regularly reinstates in me some modicum of faith in the Human Race. And Slashdot is a reminder that geeks come in all shapes and sizes, be they rotund and balding or pre-pubescent and woefully perplexed. Gizmodo taught me you could rant and rave in an informed manner about gadgets and be interesting, funny even. Fark taught me making fun of anything and everything really is actually kind of a healthy way to deal with the whole "world falling apart around us" issue. "Game Politics"and "The Ludologist" let me approach my favorite past-time with a little bit of intellectual credibility, while Penny Arcade let's me laugh while flipping the proverbial bird at nay-sayers (Yay for Child's Play!)...

We've spent this semester exploring the "HyperText" (still not sure if capitalization is any sort of element in the title... but it just feels right) as a sort of evolution in the way literature is digested by the masses. I for one don't think electronic media is going to replace good old fashioned paper-bound text any time in the near or even far future, for the same reason "real" DJs still spin Vinyls, "real" books eventually get published as a... well... book, etc. Mayhaps the old-habits-die-hard thing is in the mix there but there's something to be said about holding a physical object versus operating a scroll wheel. Or maybe electronic paper will take care of that. Who knows. I personally am a notorious early adopter so sign me up for the first cyber-bodysuit and etc. Nevertheless, the many ways in which technology and the like have influenced culture and the world as we know it provide an exciting forecast of what's to come. Maybe the next "Moby Dick" will feature multiple endings, or maybe the next "Scarface" will feature micromanaging cocaine-cartel business practices resulting in a Tony Montana who "see's the light" and builds an orphanage or runs for President, base on the story arc the audience navigates too. Better, or worse? Whose to decide. Guess we'll know when we see it on the big High-Definition Plasma Float Screen in our Living Domes.

As a final thought, The Ultimate Showdown is truly the great cinematic / musical masterpiece of our generation.
