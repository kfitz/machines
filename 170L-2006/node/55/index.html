Technology and the Future

Submitted by tophat1 on 12 September 2006 - 9:53pm.
  • blogs

When we split up in class on Monday, my partner and I touched on the subject of technology's history and future. Sometime, I believe, in the 1800's someone claimed that everything had been invented. Of course, in today's world, where new ideas and inventions are constantly emerging, we think this line is ridiculous. I would like to propose the following question: Although we may not reach a point at which all technology ceases to advance, is there a point at which it ceases to improve, or at least improve with respect to our quality of life? This question is relevant in other fields of technology as well. For example, recent debates over human engineering, eugenics and cloning have gotten us to wonder whether these new technologies are helping or hurting our society. Applied to our searches into the blogging community, the question raises issues of how helpful or useful new online inventions really are. For example, I recently found the site GoFugYourself. It is a fairly clever site that provides commentary on celebrities and their clothing,dating, etc habits. In recent blog entries, people have sent in extremely hateful messages concerning the content of the blog. If the blog spurred social commentary, it could be useful but I have recently noticed that some people simply use blogs as a therapeutic device for unconstructive anger release.

I know I'm running the risk

Submitted by marmalade on 13 September 2006 - 12:02pm.

I know I'm running the risk of getting mowed down by McLuhan when I say this (i.e. his sarcastic so "if the slugs reach the right people the firearms are good" (11) comment)--but doesn't it have something to do with content? Or (in this case) how the technology is used?

All of your examples--human engineering, eugenics, cloning--also have the power to do an incredible amount of healing. Human engineering allows parents to prevent passing down an undesirable gene (i.e. one leading to a disease or physical defect for example) to their children, and modern eugenics is technically the same thing as genetic engineering. Further, cloning has a million possibilities--from the creation of replacement organs free from the risk of rejection to the regeneration of damaged neural tissues.

Yes, all of these technologies can be negative. But they can also be overwhelmingly positive. It all depends upon how they are used. The same goes for blogs/internet technologies. We are human, and we use our technology humanly--filling them with both good and ill "content."

I don't know if that was really your question, and I fear that my answer might be quite trite as well, but I must say that--whether you eventually decide that the blog is inherenetly good or bad--isn't the fact that we're using a blog to have this discussion pretty telling? (See Ong and determinism for more... ;) )
