Perfectionists beware

Submitted by silversprung on 11 September 2006 - 10:44am.
  • the nature of the blog

I've decided, as a recovering perfectionist, that blogging is a medium that is ill-suited to people with perfectionist tendencies. I was just reading over my last post, and I found myself wanting to go back and change things about it-- add adjectives, elaborate further on a point, etc. And I have this impulse even though I previewed (and changed) the post 4 or 5 times before I actually posted it. I know that I could still change it, but I think Professor Fitzpatrick can see the changes we make, and it would be embarrassing to have her see that an entire edit consisted of adding one unnecessary phrase to a post. So I restrain myself from additional minor editing. (Except now I've outed myself.) I guess perfection is the price of immediacy, and of the rush of instantaneous publishing.

A question: do those of you who consider yourselves perfectionists also have the impulse to revise your posts? And those of you who consider yourselves on the spontaneous end of the spectrum: does blogging feel like a natural, comfortable way to express yourself?

*clink clink* go the pennies

Submitted by black lace on 11 September 2006 - 11:34am.

My 2 cents is go back & edit as many times as you see fit, even if you think others might find it a superfluous edit, because this is a very personal sort of medium (and I'd like to believe the professor has more on her mind than just why someone felt the need to change "For instance" to "To illustrate this point" or something like that, and that she won't judge us for it. That's part of the...well, the beauty of the medium, you can go back and change it whenever you see fit).

I see blogs more of an extension of the mind, and while you do need to filter it a little, total stream of consciousness doesn't always work terribly well, if it makes it clearer to you, or sounds better to you, or you think it will explain yourself better to others, go for it. Personally, I tend to edit in and out phrases a fair amount. If it's a major change, usually an addition of significant text, I'll mark it as "EDIT:" & sometimes include a time, mostly depending on my mood.

I don't really know where I fall on the spectrum. In my personal blog, an lj, I keep different grammer and mechanics than I do here (for one, I've had to go back and capitalize every single "i" in this) because the flow of the thoughts and the way the words look on the screen is very important to me in that medium. This needs to be a little more formal, so a different style & mentality (Though I still feel very talky here & probably don't punctuate as clearly and as "correctly" as I should). The purpose of each post determines how I edit it. Some are heavily worked over and edited, some are full of typos and don't entirely make sense. For me, though, each is relatively perfect for the situation and what i mean to convey, as opposed to Perfect.

Editing my personal blog is

Submitted by Anonymous on 11 September 2006 - 12:11pm.

Editing my personal blog is something I've done only occasionally. I always feel weird about it--like it's sort of a betrayal of what's unique to the medium. If a blog--particularly one of a personal journaly nature--is the blogger spouting off whatever they're feeling or thinking, what does the editing process do to that? (Especially since entries are dated?) There's something sort of pure about blogging that I like that is damaged, I think, by going back and saying, "hmm, I wish I had phrased that a little better."

This kind of a blog, for example, is a good place to brainstorm...so I feel like adding to a post would be a productive use of the blog, but deleting or changing phrases or whatever, maybe not so much.

(maybe you can tell...I'm also a perfectionist. but in a different way, heh.)
