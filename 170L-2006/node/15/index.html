[insert witty title here]

Submitted by grumpymutt on 5 September 2006 - 9:41pm.
  • blogs

Imagine walking / moving and drinking something from a wide-mouth container at the same time. i suck at that. In cars, i always pick the absolute worst time to take that first fateful sip--a sudden start and i'm suddenly coated in a cold wet layer of sugary sticky goodness. Or with walking, there's the persistent far-too-much-liquid-sloshing-at-my-face with the jolt of each footfall. That's sort of what my "blogging" experience has consisted off, trying to do too many things at once: sharing information whilst unsucessfully splicing in a bit of personal reflection with the end result being a rather unsavory batch of... well... b*tching and moaning.

Ahem. i am however still a fan of the whole blogging thing, being a notorious early-adopter if i do say so myself. i did the Xanga thing back in high school for a year or two, showed up to college and tried a sort of roll-your-own with a website before succumbing to the beast that is Blogger. Kind of ran out of steam with that one though; something about ranting about life the universe and everything to an anonymous audience that just sounded vaguely vainglorious et wot.

Blogs of note: To be honest, i'm kind of suprised no one's mentioning Boing Boing as of yet, a phenomal source of all things wonderful. Though i am noticing now the feedroll in the bottom right--fancy that. For the... technologically inclined / curious, there's one of my personal favorites, being Slashdot or "/." which is kind of funny to try to tell someone about via conversation: "Yeah. Its called Slash dot. A slash, and a dot. Period. Whatever." Yeah... "News for nerds, stuff that matters," is the site's tagline, but lots of interesting political and social debate bumbling about alongside the various news stories and etc. Fark is another good bet, though mostly in the vein of not "work-safe." For whimiscal political commentary (extremely left-leaning, to be sure) there's Fafblog, vaguely nonsensical at times but for the most part absurdly cynical and the like. Gizmodo is also the awesome if you're into shiny new toys with buttons and etc on them. Engadget too i suppose, though i prefer the former. And Kotaku and Joystiq if you're at all interested in matters of the... err... interactive entertainment media world. And if you like tinkering with things (though most of the projects go far over my head) there's Hack-A-Day which posts a ton of techy DIY (do-it-yourself, for the uninitiated... heh.) on a quasi daily basis. i've been tinkering around with a DIY projector myself, though i've no clue what the heck i'd need a projector for, 'sides cool factor of course.

Hrmm... that about does it for me.
