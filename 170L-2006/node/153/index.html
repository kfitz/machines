nut gatherers?

Submitted by marmalade on 2 October 2006 - 3:41pm.
  • stuff

OK--so, at Prof. Fitzpatrick's suggestion, I began reading McKenzie Wark's GAM3R 7H30RY recently. I'm not sure I'm on board with all of his gaming theory (he makes a lot of brouhaha over topology vs. topography and algorithim vs. allegorithim and is very fond of the word agon--a lot of which just seems cleverness, designed to impress... but I digress). Anyway, the most interesting part has actually been the user commentary.

I found the following exchange particularly interesting for a variety of reasons (as a disclaimer, its content is not representative of most of the rest of the commentary on the site):

"simon
posted: 5/25/2006

"while I read this my wife sleeps in front of the TV. My lover/mistress flirted with it but had no time or patienceâ€¦women don't interact with the technolgy in teh same way. For them its slower, the clock cycles don't tick so fast Nut gatherers and huntersâ€¦all this technology and we are only just out of the jungle

"adeola responds to simon
posted: 7/27/2006

"nut gatherers? you can't be serious.

"McKenzie Wark responds to adeola
posted: 7/28/2006

"I don't buy the nut gatherers thing either. It's just not supportedf much by archaeology or anthropology. BUt there is something of a gender difference in how people respond to games."

So, obviously I'd like to think of myself as a bit more ... evolutionarily advanced?... than a nut-gatherer. But that's a bit beside the point. How many video-game addicts do you know that are women? I know a lot of guys that would marry their xbox if they could, but not that many women who make a similar exchange. I started thinking about htis a bit after Shock and Awe's post (below) on gaming--what it means to like a fantasty world, etc. But I guess my main interest in this exchange has to do with this sort of technology-enforced gender divide.

Like Wark says, different sexes respond to gaming differently. Doesn't this apply to a lot of technology in general? I guess what I'm sort of ruminating upon is whether or not with the "death of the book"--i.e. the "technologizing of the word"--there is going to be an accompanying "death of female authorship"? Is that even valid to postulate? I don't know. What would such pure technology-driven authorship look like? Once again, I've no idea (yet?).

But I definitely think someone should knock some sense into that Simon fellow. slower cycles... gathering nuts... hmm....

gender roles in gaming

Submitted by Lulu on 3 October 2006 - 12:34am.

Wow. Definitely an idea to ponder about. I don't know the answer, but that's something that I've always wondered about. Why are there so few female gamers? And along a similar vein, why are there so few female math/science majors? Why are there so few female Mudders? Why do we see these niches as so "male"? I always find that these kinds of discussions sometimes end up stereotyping and essentializing women, and I just get confused or angry and unsure of where to go. My friend thinks it's because most video games involve lots of shooting, killing, and violence in general, and he says it's basically more male-oriented. Hmmm...maybe we should start thinking about what kind of values society has towards men and women, and what society deems as appropriate for different genders. I think there are larger forces at work here that pressure men and women away from or towards certain things...and gaming is another one of those gendered fields.

Thanks for posting this, marmalade. Definitely worth thinking about.
