the joys of tabs

Submitted by thisismycheese on 27 September 2006 - 12:44pm.
  • technology

Using Internet Explorer in this class has reminded me of a fairly recent Big Thing in browsing--tabs!

Oh, how I love tabs. I find it hard to work without them, as evidenced by the 589823953 windows I currently have open.

But it got me thinking about what some of the readings for this week talked about--namely, the complaints that if you're on LotsOfLinks.com, you have to keep hitting the "back" button after you've visited each link to get back to the original list of links.

Which tabs fix, really, since you just control-click the link and it opens it in a tab, and you can load tons of tabs at the same time, and it's amazing and great. Clearly Somebody Up There agreed with Joyce that having to go back and forth was really inefficient.

I mean, when I'm working at my computer, I'll usually have two or three windows open--one for each class, one for fun stuff, whatever--and each window could have one tab or, you know, twenty. But it organizes things really well.

Anyway, that's my ode to tabs. I like them. And I like that they organize my web browsing experience in a way that makes a lot of sense and doesn't involve a ton of backtracking. And while tabs aren't the same as Joyce and Landow's utopic vision of an internet where everyone is linking back and forth to the appropriate places in documents, etc., it's a pretty good user-side approximation.
