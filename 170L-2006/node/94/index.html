Question

Submitted by marmalade on 19 September 2006 - 4:01pm.
  • the nature of the blog

Maybe this is just a silly question, but isn't there something more to "writing machines" than the blog?

Not that the blog isn't fascinating and exciting and way cool (etc) but it strikes me that maybe we've been beating it a bit to death these last couple of weeks.

This has been sort of running around in my head, esp in relationship with our projected wiki. I guess I just feel that there must be more out there. I mean, is the blog the end all and be all of online writing? Sure, we've tossed around a couple of NYTimes articles, and our choices from the blog spectrum have been pretty broad (everything from the blogs of lawyers and politicians to collections of stuff like postsecret), but we seem to be circling around this one great big grail that is the blog.

I think magoo has been onto some great stuff with his stuff on visual poetry and this discussion of Shelley Jackson's very weird publishing stunt. But (and I'm thinking especially about all of the tattooed people here) this isn't even really what I would call contemporary fiction. It's just ... well, a stunt.

So I guess this is sort of a question for all of you who know way more about this stuff than I--but is there another brick that's going to fall? Is there something more to online writing than the blog? Or am I just holding out in vain?

And (if the answer is no--there's just the blog, marmalade you're a great big idiot), maybe we could speculate a bit about if that's going to be the case for the future. You know, pull some very prescient Vannevar Bush moves or something. And, who knows, there might be some cool ideas that come up for wiki stuff...?

That is a great question, and I echo your concerns. I was just thinking today about how we were going to write about blogging for an entire semester. Because I am part of the

Submitted by thisismycheese on 19 September 2006 - 8:52pm.

Because I am part of the Nerd Contingent, and because I'm taking a class in it, umm, if we're looking for forms of writing that have undergone drastic changes since the advent of the internet--there's always fanfiction. It existed prior to the internet, but it's become vastly more visible now that it's not just circulated in fanzines among little clumps of Star Trek fans. Now tons of random people who've never seriously written are before churning out novel-length works. Like blogs, it's democratizing to an extent. It also brings in a lot of issues that blogging doesn't (like how far we're willing to take copyright law on the internet). It's a community/almost-genre that has become largely internet-based, and that uses the internet in some interesting ways.

I also think the ebook is sort of an interesting deal--right now no one really cares about them (and some people, myself included, sort of resent them), but I feel like there must be some cool possibilities if the technology gets better. Google talked about scanning in all the books a while back--what if all books became wikified, and you could just click a word or reference and it would link you through to a billion other things that would explain it? (It would make Gravity's Rainbow an easier read, at the least.)
...anyway, I'm just making stuff up.
