Wiki Comment/Question

Submitted by tophat1 on 15 October 2006 - 4:07pm.
  • wiki

I just visited the site planning page of the wiki and realized that it's not a very active page (at least not in recent history). So, I'm sorry in advance to clog blog space with wiki discussion, BUT...

Night Owl and I were discussing the blog last Wednesday during class and we returned to our initial idea of a frame story. I'm not sure to what extent we've achieved a frame story as of right now, but I think it might be a useful way of perpetuating the story on the first page (I'm not sure what the technical term is). I was thinking it might be cool if we have a bit more of a setting for our story (ie. maybe we're all stuck in a room or building at school, or we're on some sort of trip in a vehicle). That way, we might set a bit more of a tone for the stories, or at least for the characters who are telling our stories. Also, we would have more of a meta-story to narrate throughout the telling of our other stories.

Again, this is just a thought that you may or may not find useful. I think we've done some great work, I just thought I would throw out another perspective on what's going on and I didn't want to do anything directly on the wiki until I got some feedback. Thanks!

frame or effect?

Submitted by crashingintowalls on 17 October 2006 - 11:43am.

I agree that things get dispersed rather quickly once you move through the front page, but I'm not sure if it is necessarily the case that this isn't a storytelling device in itself. Granted, I have been a part of the weird (and not necessarily effective) frontpage, so perhaps this is just me being defensive, but I feel that adding a frame in medias res could be misleading. Of course, the fact that we can do this at all is an interesting quality of this kind of literature...

If there is a single question in my muddled post, it is whether we actually need a frame. If it is useful to our ongoing project, then that is a good thing. However, I want to make sure that this is a functional move rather than an application of a convention that does not have to be applicable here.
