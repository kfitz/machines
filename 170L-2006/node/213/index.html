Hypertext fiction, Electronic literature

Submitted by magoo on 14 October 2006 - 4:25pm.

You have a point. The whole school of words-just-as-symbols or everything as language gets away from even its originators. Derrida, for instance, nowhere indicates that statements cannot be made, stories cannot be told, only that such things and our understanding of them is problematized. All these things -- including Finnegans Wake (see the blog entry just after yours) or Lexia are props to deal with human percept or human issues, however well or poorly.

Maybe more to the point: no language, no art, no perception happens without form, but all these symbols or signs have reference, too.

I think that in most cases, people who discuss form don't intend to deny that, though I suppose exceptions might come to mind.
