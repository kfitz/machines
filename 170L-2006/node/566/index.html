101 vexations

Submitted by a bird on 6 December 2006 - 12:27am.
  • Semester projects

Does anybody else feel like they've bitten off way more than they can chew regarding their term project? Because by the looks of things on Monday, half of you seem mostly DONE, which is amazing to me. I have my 101 lexia staring at me, and while I know every story that's going in the remaining ones, it's just....daunting.
And then there are the lexia I don't know what to do with, because I have too many stories for that person.
It's just hard with some of the people, like I'll never get across exactly what I want to for them unless the reader knows them themselves. With a fictional hypertext, everything (or mostly everything) about it is on paper, or screen (whatever). Unless it's part of a massive series you're doing elsewhere, this is all of it. Not so with an actual human. Let alone 101. I can only give you the vaguest idea of who they are, and that upsets me. I think that's why I wanted to retain their names - it's another part of identity I can give.

oz's word "hypervexed" is

Submitted by Lulu on 7 December 2006 - 1:35am.

oz's word "hypervexed" is definitely cool.

and i felt the same way that you do. the thing about hypertexts that i've learned through building my own as well as working on the class wiki is that they tend to grow exponentially and by nature are supposed to grow this way, and often without an end. the best way to deal with this intimidating fact is to just sit oneself down and pound it all out. i usually am not very good with planning a project out and find that i am most productive when i just sit and start typing--that's when all my ideas rush out. i don't know if it's the same for you, but sometimes that's the best strategy--especially when you're pressed for time.

good luck finishing your 101 lexias!
