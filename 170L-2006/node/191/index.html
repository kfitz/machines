Survey

Submitted by Natwwal on 9 October 2006 - 2:01pm.
  • blogs
  • wiki

So we've been working on two forms of electronic literature for this class: this blog and our wiki. I'm wondering how people would compare the two experiences. Is one harder or easier for you? Do you enjoy one more than the other? Any other random thoughts?

I find that I'm a lot less inhibited about adding to the wiki than I am about writing on this blog. Maybe it's the fact that the wiki is a work in progress, or the fact that I don't have to slap my name on my wiki contributions. I'm not entirely sure. It's somewhat ironic, because I've never taken a creative writing class in my life. I'm curious about how other people feel about this.

I'm also far more

Submitted by Lulu on 9 October 2006 - 6:28pm.

I'm also far more comfortable on the blog. I think this mostly has to do with the fact that there's structure and clarity to it. Like magoo, I also feel like I'm shooting blind on the wiki. Even though it's already halfway through the semester, I feel like our wiki is still a work in progress, and right now, I'm pretty anxious about whether it will start evolving from "work in progress" to a "work" with some sort of collective, coherent identity. For me, it's harder to be creative on the wiki when I'm not sure how my contributions are moving the bigger project forward. There's nothing stopping me from changing the wiki however I like, but that would not be very helpful or productive to the project as a whole--since it's a collective narrative.

So I guess this discussion kind of reminds me of the book/hypertext dichotomy. Like that parallel, I'm more comfortable with the traditional structures than with novelty. Hmm...maybe this makes me a country bumpkin even though I'm from the city...
