blogs I now read.

Submitted by a bird on 6 December 2006 - 12:19am.
  • blogs

This class has changed my everyday life more than any other class I've taken...ever, I think. Why? Because now, instead of reading online news, or listening to music, I compulsively check blogs. All the freaking time. And I love it.
The funny thing is, though, the blogs I read. They're all related, all blogrolled on each other, which is how I found them. The way I'd describe most of them is....well, Asian-American daddy blogs. I know. That's really weird.
I would say I have no idea how I ended up being a total lurker on these blogs, but I actually do. It's Tom Cruise's fault, really. When that Vanity Fair came out with the pictures of Suri, I was interested, and looked it up online. This is how I found my way to the Celebrity Baby Blog, which is, I've discovered, a much cuter way to get my pop culture news than People.
After a while, I got annoyed with them for not updating every 10 minutes, so I checked out their blogroll. I stumbled across Perez Hilton's Hollywood blog, and was pretty freaked out and disgusted, so I clicked on something a little less innocuous sounding from the CBB blogroll - something called MetroDad. And this is where I discovered the first of my As-Am (yes, I have the lingo, too) daddies. This one is amazingly written, sarcastic as hell, and peppered with stories about his 2 year old daughter. Just what I like (If you can't tell, I really like babies).
Then MetroDad started up this OTHER blog site with a bunch of his AsAm daddy bloggers, in the spirit of Kimchi Mamas, the AsAm mommy blogging community. The Rice Daddies are a whole community of dads who happen to be Asian American, all contributing regularly to the group blog. And of course, from there, I followed them to their own blogs, such as the Jewish-Asian-American writer's Daddy in a Strange Land.
What I really love about these blogs is that they all comment on each other's blogs, and it really is a true community. Their kids all know each other now, and there are often pictures of them hanging out - and even better, most of the dads have wives blogging on Kimchi Mamas and their own blogs too. It's really like a big old hypertext of acquaintances and friends and relatives. It's really interesting to read.

And that is how I became addicted to Asian-American daddy blogs.

i've definitely started

Submitted by Lulu on 7 December 2006 - 1:31am.

i've definitely started looking at more blogs since taking this class. i tend to see it as informing myself with news rather than seeing it as another form of procrastination.

in addition to the added blogs in my life, i've also found myself reading the technology section of newspapers more now and getting excited whenever there is a video game review or something about blogs in there.

so yeah, i totally know what you mean when you say that this class has drastically changed your everyday life. it's done the same for me in a very positive way.
