my best friend is a blog

Submitted by Frabby on 28 November 2006 - 5:27pm.
  • blogs

Shock and Awe's post about someone mistaking his class project for a real person's blog made me think about a blog that my friend told me about. It's from a Texas cop, and consists of his ramblings and thoughts about law enforcement, America, guns, and his own work. It's written in this tough, good ol' boy persona, and is unsurprisingly super conservative. Of course, my friend and I were having this conversation on AIM, and in the spirit of our class, here is a brief excerpt-

Friend (4:00:45 PM): i just spent three days reading this guy's blog start to finish
Murplay (4:00:19 PM): what blog?
Friend (4:01:41 PM): http://texas-music.blogspot.com/, he's a Texan cop, he was working as an undercover narc for most of it
Murplay (4:02:19 PM): how can he put that online?
Friend (4:03:04 PM): well he changes names and details, and doesnt talk about some stuff at all
Friend (4:03:22 PM): he writes some fiction too
Murplay (4:03:22 PM): hmm
Murplay (4:03:33 PM): okay
Friend (4:04:04 PM): he's my new buddy
Murplay (4:03:37 PM): hahah
Murplay (4:03:38 PM): how so?
Friend (4:04:18 PM): haha, he just is. i think he's awesome

After hearing such high praise, I decided to check it out myself. I was not impressed, and I couldn't handle reading that much of it because I think this guy kind of sounds like an asshole. But I also got the sense that this was highly fictionalized, more than what my friend seems to think. If it is mostly fiction, than it's a pretty sad time for him. I don't think this guy is participating in a grand experiment in internet fiction, but just trying to look coolâ€¦
I'm also kind of amused (or maybe disturbed) that my pal is so captivated by this man's ramblings, and may have to inquire further.

Hmm..I wonder how much the "A-list" bloggers also lie on their blogs. I wonder how often they make things up in order to sound interesting. I've been bothered by the fact that there is even an A-list since the beginning of the semester. It's the ultimate form of narcissism. Besides, these people aren't even the most interesting people in the world...why do they deserve all our attention? I say we boycott.

what to do with these people

Submitted by tophat1 on 29 November 2006 - 5:21pm.

I also agree that there are many blogs out there that seem pretty bad (and possibly false). Of course, it is also often the case, as it seems to be with the Texas cop, that the ones that are bad are obviously bad. The best we can do is, as lulu suggests, decide not to read them. Ultimately, it seems hard to know how to decide what is worth reading and not worth reading without checking it out yourself. Word of mouth is unreliable and the A-lists of bloggers is, well, ridiculous.

blogs, blog fiction, Truth

Submitted by magoo on 30 November 2006 - 4:53pm.

I'm trying, and have been trying, to find some unstupid way of asking "What is truth in a blog?" I'm not saying the word's not valid, but I wonder. So this guy writes cop stuff. Is it true if:

  • He's sincere but grossly mistaken?
  • He writes realistically, describing details in a perceptive fashion, but changes names and places?
  • The overarching concepts by which he organizes his narrative can be taken as valid?
  • Every word he writes is true, but he leaves things out that are clearly relevant?
  • Every word he writes is true, but he leaves things out that are only moderately relevant?
  • Every word he writes is true, but he leaves things out which by his own ideology appear irrelevant, although they may be relevant in another organization of the story, which is, however, only partially correct itself?

Well, you could take this as far as you like, I suppose. I feel naive to say so, but I'm still a little worried that Truth depends on which medium someone assumes I'm writing in, especially since that context will be reclassified as other media develop.
