Skin and Elitism

Submitted by thenewblack on 5 November 2006 - 10:49pm.
  • authorial tyranny
  • Skin

I am almost unnerved by how furious the basic conceit of Skin makes me. Apart from the cult-like nature of Jackson's recruitment methods (Hey kids! Get an inane tattoo, in book font mind you, and I'll tell you the real story!), the idea of a text that you basically need to join a club to read is the height of elitism. I suppose if one accepts the whole indie mentality of obscurity as the highest good, then one might be inclined to be really excited about this. Not that I really have any right to tell Shelley Jackson how to express herself, but it seems to me that the goal of art should be to spread one's work as far as possible, to reach as many people as possible. I find it hilarious that Shelley Jackson has managed to practically pull this off, and is getting attention for an experiment without any accessible content to back it up. Let's just hope that the scientologists don't catch wind of this one. That said, the one thing that I do admire about Skin is the way that the "words" are free to express their own interpretations of their tattoos on Skin's website. At least they get some input here.

However, that brings me to the thing that really pisses me off. This has already been discussed on the blog, but seriously, how dehumanizing is this whole referring to people as "words" business. Also, I am apalled by the way that Jackson basically takes ownership of these people, saying, "the author will do all in her power to attend the funerals of her words" and "they are not understood as carriers, or agents of the words that they bear, but as their embodiments." These people are then meant to embody a randomly assigned word just because Jackson says so? I'd be really interested to talk to some of these people, just to find out what the hell made this seem attractive. Also, I'm disgusted by Jackson's imposition of stylistic guidelines. These people have basically agreed to serve Jackson's concept, at their own expense I'm assuming, and yet they're still meant to adhere to Jackson's idea of what they should put on their goddamn bodies. This insistence on stylistic unity in a project where all of the participants are never seen together strikes me as needlessly dictatorial and insulting. Without the volunteers, there's not text, so why not let them use whatever font they like? Skin is very interesting (look how long I've been ranting), but in the same sense that reading about the Manson family is interesting.

For those who have slogged through all this vitriol, here's a picture of a kitten. Enjoy!

Elitism

Submitted by Frabby on 6 November 2006 - 10:44am.

"Skin" is certainly elitist in the sense that privileges for a certain group (the participants and Jackson) over others. But I don't think it's elitist in terms of all the baggage that the word usually carries. In fact, Jackson has taken theory and put it into practice and included people not usually part of the academic disucssions. As I have said before, I think the project brings up interesting questions about stories/texts/immortality of words. And though the narrative will never be available to us, the actual plot seems a bit irrelevant.

Also, if you look around on Jackson's website, it's clear she has a sense of humor about the whole thing. She's linked to another site that parodies her project, so though I haven't met her, I have to imagine that she isn't completely full of herself.

Her language regarding the "words" is certainly off-putting. I suppose I read that with the understanding that Jackson didn't truly believe that these people were no longer human. It's just a project! It's just a tattoo (that these people chose to get, obviously no one is supporting forced tattooing a la the Holocaust)!

Perhaps I'm biased because I have a tattoo and the experience was not life-defining. I can see how the project could look different if you had strong opinions about tattoos.

p.s. Jackson herself has tattoos (or so Google Images suggests...)

You're right,...

Submitted by thenewblack on 6 November 2006 - 10:03pm.

...I do have strong opinions about tattoos. While I do want a tattoo (or two or three), I don't have any yet. Why? Because I'm not going to put anything on my body impulsively, and I'm sure as hell not going to adhere to someone else's vision when I do. It took me almost a year to actually get my piercings in after I decided I wanted them, and I imagine the whole tattoo thing could take even longer. Given these opinions on body mod, I doubt my post should come as any kind of a surprise anymore. What I find so creepy about Skin is that people are volunteering to have their bodies colonized by someone else's vision. And if they dig it, then more power to them. It wouldn't be my choice, but it's their right. However, I don't think that the enthusisasm of the participants obliges me to respect the project, which I still do not. While Jackson may have a sense of humor about it, the fact is that she is still in the power position in this little collective, and is not shy about acknowledging it.
