Comparison/contrast

Submitted by marmalade on 9 September 2006 - 8:11am.
  • readings

I read the Ong yesterday, and I talked everyone's ear off at dinner recapitulating most of his arguments--I just thought the whole paper was exceptionally interesting. I started on the McLuhan this morning, and besides the seriously dated feeling of the material, I found it much less compelling. However, I felt they worked excellently in opposition to each other--each had a totally different conception of orality and its various merits.

The big difference that I noticed had to do with Ong's thesis of orality as being limiting. The written word for him was a revolution--and a positive one. The great works of epic fiction that we've inherited in the form of the Iliad or the Bible were only possible because they occurred just as their civilizations were on the cusp of literacy. One of his more compelling examples was the list--it is something unique to written language--a record that reminds us of things to do or of what has been done. The lists and lists of begats and the accounting for the movements of Israel through the wilderness were proof for Ong of the Bible's new "non-oral" status.

The newest New Critics

Submitted by Oz on 6 September 2006 - 1:27am.
  • the nature of the blog

"Every one asks me what I 'think' of everything," said Spencer Brydon; "and I make answer as I can--begging or dodging the question, putting them off with any nonsense. It wouldn't matter to any of them really," he went on, "for, even were it possible to meet in that stand-and-deliver way so silly a demand on so big a subject, my 'thoughts' would still be almost altogether about something that concerns only myself."
--Henry James, "The Jolly Corner"

This quotation nicely sums up a lot of my frustrations with critical discourse--both the stuff I'm being trained to produce and the stuff I read as part of that training. That said, I realize how ironic it is that the start of my first blog entry reads much more like a critical essay, but part of what I discovered while weblog-browsing is that there's quite a bit of overlap between the blogger and the critic. For the time being, I'm going to stick with the truly obvious and hope I can analyze that statement with more subtlety later on.
