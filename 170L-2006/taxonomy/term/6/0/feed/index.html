Writing Machines - wiki http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/taxonomy/term/6/0 en In which I ramble more about the wiki http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/689 <p>The problem with multiple authors is that you're inevitably going to see some push and pull. Some people want to see the story go one way, and the others think that's crazy talk. I think there's been a bit of that on the wiki this semester. However, I think the thing about electronic literature is that some inconsistency and feral growth is not a bad thing. So maybe every single dot on the wiki doesn't connect perfectly. So what. Fragmentation is inevitable, and we are essentially left with just as many potentialities as consistent eventualities.</p> <p>What I'm trying to get at here is that the wiki doesn't need to make perfect sense. It is endlessly expandable, so what does it matter if we get two versions of the same story on there, or multiple endings, or unresolved questions, or what have you. Its scope is just as large as people care to expand it, and it can encompass just as much as people care to put in.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/689">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/689#comments wiki Sat, 16 Dec 2006 04:03:04 +0000 thenewblack 689 at http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006 Wiki kudos http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/675 <p>Also, I want to reiterate how pleased I am with the way the wiki has turned out.
