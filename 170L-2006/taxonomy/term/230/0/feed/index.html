Writing Machines - puzzles http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/taxonomy/term/230/0 en Skin and Hypertext as Jigsaw Puzzles http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/371 <p>This might seem like I am stating the obvious, but is it just me, or does Shelley Jackson seem--in addition to being super interested in bodies, as <a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L/node/369">Frabby</a> pointed out--to also be obsessed with puzzles? It seems like if we were to simplify the concepts behind <em>Skin</em> and <em>Patchwork Girl</em>, then it would seem like Jackson is creating a giant jigsaw puzzle. </p> <p>When I first looked at the <a href="http://ineradicablestain.com/skin.html">frontpage</a> of Skin, I was misled into thinking that her final project was supposed to be presented as a giant collage/puzzle of the people's tattoos. <a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L/node/368">Pimm</a> also linked us to Jackson's tatooed people's <a href="http://community.livejournal.com/mortalworkofart">community blog</a>, in which someone mentions that "I am we," which seems like the individual does acknowledge that he or she is like a puzzle piece in a larger collective identity. Not to strip people of their humanity and say that they're/we're all puzzle pieces, but it does seem like Jackson's hinting at this concept with her work.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/371">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/371#comments art narratives puzzles Skin Tue, 07 Nov 2006 09:49:01 +0000 Lulu 371 at http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006
