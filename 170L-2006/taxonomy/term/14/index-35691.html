Xanga

Submitted by Lulu on 5 September 2006 - 4:15pm.
  • blogs

Most of the blogging that I've done these past few years has been on the wonderful but, admittedly, very "high-school-ish" site called Xanga. It's pronounced "zanga." :) Xanga is very much like Livejournal, where the blogger creates an account for herself and then proceeds to musing/journaling/ranting, usually about everyday encounters, and in my case, college life. I would say that about 90% of the "blogging" that I do is on xanga, and what keeps me going back is the large community of "xangans" that I keep connected to in this community. It's basically a process of reading friends' "xangas" and commenting on each other's xangas, keeping each other updated on what we're feeling, thinking, eating, etc. I'm sure xangas (yes, we call these things "xangas" instead of "blogs") are not considered legitimate according to the definition of some of the A-listers we read about in the text, but in this busy college world, these xangas are a good, convenient way of knowing what's up in each other's lives. Xanga also has features called blogrings that allow people with shared interests to join, thus connecting individuals with others who have similar tastes in a particular topic. There is even a Pomona blogring, and that is where I do most of my xanga-ing.
