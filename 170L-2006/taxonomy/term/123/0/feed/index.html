Writing Machines - literary criticism http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/taxonomy/term/123/0 en "Writing Machines", literary criticism, academia http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/194 <p>When I was reading Hayles's "Writing Machines," I couldn't help but feel skeptical about...well, to be brutally honest, the entire point. I actually agreed with and was sympathetic to a lot of Hayles' points about media ecology, the bathtub theory of literature, and the continual evolution and enhancement of books alongside the progression of electronic literature. In fact, I really loved when she said "as we work toward critical practices and theories appropriate for electronic literature, we may come to renewed appreciation for the specificity of print." (33) However, despite all these great points that I could sympathize with, I was also skeptical about a lot of things, and in particular her analysis of electronic literature like "Lexia to Perplexia." I'm not critical as to the actual content or analysis. Instead, I guess my discomfort stems from a lack of clarity as to who the audience is, and the actual value of literary criticism. So, I guess this entry isn't just a straightforward critique of an assigned reading. It's more a pondering of why I am reading what I'm reading and the usefulness of what I'm learning.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/194">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/194#comments literary criticism Mon, 09 Oct 2006 23:45:05 +0000 Lulu 194 at http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006
