Writing Machines - Jenkins http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/taxonomy/term/153/0 en Micronarratives and Background Information http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/250 <p>In "Game Design as Narrative Architecture," Henry Jenkins talks about narrative presence, in the form of previously encountered narratives as well as micronarratives, in games. He describes certain games that involve previous knowledge. His cites "American McGee's Alice" as an example of a game derived from an older story (Lewis Carroll's "Alice in Wonderland"). The premise of the game is strange: years later Alice is trying to figure out of her experience of Wonderland was real or a hallucination. Jenkins makes the point that the gamer plays against a background of previous encounters with "storybook illustrations and Disney movies." Yet, it would be quite different to draw from the Disney movie or storybook vs. Lewis Carroll's book. The sinister feel of the game would be in much starker contrast to the Disney movie.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/250">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/250#comments Jenkins Sun, 22 Oct 2006 23:58:48 +0000 tophat1 250 at http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006
