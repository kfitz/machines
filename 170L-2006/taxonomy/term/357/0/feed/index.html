Writing Machines - plagiarism http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/taxonomy/term/357/0 en Pynchon <3's Plagiarism http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/645 <p>I haven't really been following the Ian McEwan plagiarism ordeal, but I happened upon (i.e. Google News pointed me toward) <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/main.jhtml;jsessionid=53WGFN1XUOGXFQFIQMFSFFOAVCBQ0IV0?xml=/news/2006/12/05/nwriters105.xml">this article in the UK's Daily Telegraph</a>. McEwan has been accused of plagiarizing multiple portions of a WWII nurse's memoir for his 2002 book <em>Atonement</em>. </p> <p>Plagiarism is obviously a rich issue, but the main point I want to raise here is about its relationship to advancing technology. It's easy to imagine a future in which plagiarism (at least of the physical, word-for-word kind) would be impossible to get away with, for any amount of time. Not only will there be more people accessing and commenting upon written works, but, as more and more is digitized, there seems the opportunity for immediate, thorough and computerized cross-referencing. Will we then search more vigorously for writers to ostracize for plagiarism of ideas?</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/645">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/645#comments links plagiarism Fri, 15 Dec 2006 10:55:49 +0000 night owl 645 at http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006
