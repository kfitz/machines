It takes the best attributes of the Guardian -- great writing, focused arguments, and a classy presentation -- and combines them with the best attributes of the blog -- astonishing timeliness, personal perspective, and an open forum for commentary. The contributors are not constrained in the way they might be by their bosses and editors in the process of writing articles for the Guardian proper, but their approach is still uniform and stable. Entries fall under prominent headings, as in "Art," "Film," "Music," etc. They have cover images and subheadlines. And, in general, the entries contain well-reasoned, specific arguments.
