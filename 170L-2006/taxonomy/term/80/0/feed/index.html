McLuhan makes a big deal out of the idea that media provide us with extensions of ourselves, but what if the medium is unreliable? I'm concerned only about the mechanics of the medium, not about the misrepresentation of any "real" world. I mean what happens when you reach for something and it's just not <a href="http://www.lakdnflsdkfponekdl.com">there</a>? We don't tolerate books with missing pages, or television signals that spontaneously drop segments from shows. How many times has class in ITS been momentarily derailed by a misdirected browser?
