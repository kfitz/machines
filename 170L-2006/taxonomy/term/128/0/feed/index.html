GAM3R 7H30RY, I felt a similar tension--between linear and nonlinear modes of reading. In GAM3R 7H30RY, you read the text itself, on those digital index card-looking things, and then you have a choice about whether or not to read the comments. Now, it seems that the comments are often the most interesting part of the text, so you really shouldn't skip them. On the other hand, the importance of the comments doesn't seem to lessen the value of Wark's text, because without the original text, the comments wouldn't exist.
