Writing Machines - daily life http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/taxonomy/term/358/0 en More and More (Media)tion http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/646 <p>As if we needed even more technology to mediate the world for us, someone has come up with <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2006/12/10/magazine/10section1A.t-3.html">the ambient walkman</a>. Basically, the product is a pair of headphones which sample ambient noise and turn it into some form of music. Its creator prefers to style it "The Ambient Addition," which frankly I find insufferable. The idea that we need even more mediation seems ridiculous to me. I suppose that the technology represents a somewhat cool achievement, but I'm at a loss to understand why anyone would want to listen to an approximation of music created by the birds, the bees, and those jackhammers down the street (although I suppose that combination would be pretty wild).</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/646">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/646#comments daily life media technology Fri, 15 Dec 2006 13:02:44 +0000 thenewblack 646 at http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006
