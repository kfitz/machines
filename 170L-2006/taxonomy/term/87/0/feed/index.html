Writing Machines - Classes http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/taxonomy/term/87/0 en A small thought http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/120 <p>As I read through Joyce's "Othermindedness" or rather the selection thereof, I'm intrigued by the connections that he makes between the filtration systems of the internet (Google, "metasites," etc.) and the filtrations that occur in the "print culture." He writes, "Almost invisibly in the past, for instance, most library patrons read much more of the online or card catalog entries, book spines, or tables of contents than they read from the volumes themselves. People have only so much time. They can't read everything and so they depend on others to link them to what they need or wish to read" (54). It seems then that according to Joyce, mediation has always been a factor in the way that we receive information, but this mediation has never been so overt as in the case of the internet.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/120">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/120#comments books Classes Michael Joyce Mon, 25 Sep 2006 04:33:06 +0000 thenewblack 120 at http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006
