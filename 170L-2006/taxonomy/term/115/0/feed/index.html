N. Katherine Hayles mentions two things that struck me as perfect examples of how technology not only affects, but also in a way creates, its users/readers.</p> <p>The first example is the computer itself. Hayles writes: "In their general form, computers are simulation machines producting <em>environments</em>, from objects that sit on desktops to networks spanning the globe. To construct an environment is, of course, to anticipate and structure the user's interaction with it and in this sense to construct the user as well as the interface" (48). This makes a lot of sense to me.
