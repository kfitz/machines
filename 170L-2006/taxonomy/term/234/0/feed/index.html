Writing Machines - networks http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/taxonomy/term/234/0 en Library Thing http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/374 <p>I know that we're going to be looking at networked writing next week, and this website falls under that, but I'm going to jump the gun and talk about it anyways--because I am kind of disconcerted about my experience with <a href="http://www.librarything.com/">LibraryThing</a></p> <p>I feel like somebody talked about a similar website earlier, where you could look at other people's catalogued favorite music/books/movie, but I can't find the post, so sorry if this is a bit repetitive.</p> <p>But to my point: I spent a bit of time with LibraryThing today, and the whole thing has left me feeling very very not special. Every time I type in a favorite book, lists of my other favorite books come up tagged as well. It's almost eerie. Evidently there are thousands of people who have read the exact same things as me and then had similar reactions. I think what you read, what you really enjoy, reflects on you as a person. So I'd like to think that I'm a little bit original, a little bit quirky.
