Writing Machines - stories http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/taxonomy/term/21/0 en Facade and Time-Travel in a Non-Deterministic World http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/252 <p>The first time I played through Facade, I was thorougly unimpressed. The characters didn't seem to respond to the things I said, and when they did respond to me, they often completely misinterpreted what I was saying. At first, I attributed these things to poor programming. However, as I played through the game again, I realized that the moods of the characters varied significantly between runthroughs. The underlying tensions were always there, but in some runthroughs they were much more reactive than others, even when little in my behavior changed. There were some events that, as far as I can tell, were entirely determined by random chance. This dynamic is, in my mind, what separates Facade from other games or interactive stories. In Facade, you have some degree of influence over the events, but there are still major plot points that change from version to version for reasons entirely outside of your control.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/252">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/252#comments games stories Mon, 23 Oct 2006 05:26:31 +0000 Natwwal 252 at http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006 Memories of Blog http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/19 <p>During our first class meetings and the intervening time,I was unable to recall a time when I had actually read a blog regularly or for its own sake.
