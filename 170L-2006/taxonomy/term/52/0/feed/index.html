Probably intentionally so. Because as much as it's about reading and rereading, it also consistently reads like a handout a professor might give to his hypertext fiction workshop. And that's probably Joyce's point: "reading in hypertext means to re-create the writers experience of rereading in the process of composing printed works" (139).
