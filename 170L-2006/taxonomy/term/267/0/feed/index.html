Writing Machines - collective memory http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/taxonomy/term/267/0 en Another collaborative authorship project courtesy of wiki software http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/426 <p>I came across a wiki-type site called <a href="//www.memoryarchive.org/en/MemoryArchive ">Memory Archive, the encyclopedia of memories</a>. The stated idea here is that: <cite>all the people, like you, who are alive today and who bore witness to the significant events of our age, will pass on. Our collective memory -- yours, mine, all of ours -- will go into the great beyond with us, vanishing forever. Over the coming decade, century, and millenium [sic], your experiences will slowly dissappear [sic]. The past, they say, is a foreign country -- distant, strange, often unknowable. Unless you have a map. Your memoirs, recorded and shared at MemoryArchive, are that map for future generations. Go ahead. Tell the future how it was to be alive now--You've got a story. Make it history.</cite> So essentially, this is a wiki software gone memory aggregator: people add "memoirs" about "the significant events of our age," and these memoirs are broken up into categories. For example, I could read all the collected memories about Barack Obama, about the year 1978, about the assassination of JFK, about bra stuffing.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/426">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/426#comments collective memory wiki software Tue, 14 Nov 2006 04:28:11 +0000 Oz 426 at http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006
