Writing Machines - squiggly lines http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/taxonomy/term/131/0 en Daunted by Design http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/208 <p>I, like magoo, was very interested in the discussion on Monday relating to our frustration with the "bad web design" of Lexia Perplexia and Disappearing Rain. Not being a web-designer myself, I was struck by a vague feeling of dissatisfaction reading the two of them. Upon reflection, I realized it was because of how aesthetically displeasing they were. I'm used to web-sites that look good, with nice color schemes, cool fonts, pictures, layout, etc. I like my websites like I like my magazines: slick.</p> <p>Certainly part of the problem is that Lexia and Rain are old, and Larsen and Memmott were working with the techonology they had at the time, but I think it raises an interesting question: In regard to hypertexts, is being a good writer not enough?
