Writing Machines - the future of books http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/taxonomy/term/268/0 en Online Books http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/427 <p>Today in class we talked about the future of the book. In particular, I was surprised that Bolter was so free in uploading "Writing Space" on the internet and allowing anyone to edit is as they please. They say once you write a book and publish it for the world, it's not really yours anymore. I guess Bolter is just really taking it to the next level. At any rate, I was wondering, outside of the community of scholars particularly interested in this subject, who else legitimate would put a book online for free?</p> <p>I found my answer on a site called www.edge.org on which Jaron Lanier writes an article called <a href="http://http://www.edge.org/discourse/digital_maoism.html">"Digital Maoism: The Hazards of the New Online Collectivism"</a>. Yochai Benkler, who is a law professor at Yale, has published a book called "The Wealth of Networks: How Social Production Transforms Markets and Freedom." He then posted it as a free download online and people (Lanier says between 15,000 and 20,000) downloaded it and added comments and links. I checked it out and, sure enough, within seconds I had all 527 pages on computer. Benkler said it's an experiment and presentation of what he sees as the future of the book.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/427">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/427#comments the future of books Tue, 14 Nov 2006 07:20:40 +0000 tophat1 427 at http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006
