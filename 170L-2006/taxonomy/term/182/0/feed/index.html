However, it occurs to me that most of our critiques of the sites have centered around the somewhat frivolous content of the stories rather than around the genre/medium itself. McLuhan would be displeased with us. It seems to me that it would be possible to create a very interesting story with this kind of format. There's no reason I can think of why these stories need to center around boys and clothes and alcohol. Alas, it seems that new literary and artistic forms often begin as either cliched, melodramatic entertainment or insufferably self-concious exercises in being different.</p> <p><a href="http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/309">read more</a></p> http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/309#comments online caroline planetjemma Mon, 30 Oct 2006 19:52:26 +0000 Natwwal 309 at http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006 Playing vs. being played http://machines.pomona.edu.170L-2006/node/304 <p>My reaction to Caroline and Jemma doesn't stand out from the rest of the class who has blogged on them: they weren't my favorite.
