  1. What is Rose's project in this book?
  2. How does she propose to go about it?
  3. What ideas does she seem to be drawing from Marxism?

Project:

  • Rap as black cultural production
  • Rap as lens to view African American culture
  • How rap affects pop culture and America as a whole (not just black culture)
  • How black culture and rap specifically are perceived by white America; rap as primary way that white Americans see black culture
  • Exploring the social context/historical development of rap as a cultural phenomenon
  • Why black culture is solely identified by music

Method:

  • Four points: history, media, sexual politics, innovations
  • Looking at rap from new angle: history usually buried
  • Social, cultural, and political implications of hip-hop culture (not just rap) on society in general
  • Polyvocal - theory and practice
  • Must make room in traditional Marxism for critiques based on race and gender

Marxism?:

  • Relationship btw. Bourgeoisie and proletariat illuminated by looking at corporate influence in rap
  • Access to technology/MTV on part of rappers; access to tapes on part of audience; importance of economics in rap production
  • Ways that product changed in response to market (esp. with introduction of white audience, MTV, etc.); not seen as legitimate art form until commodified
  • Appeal to white audience based in assuaging of guilt, romanticizing of ghetto
  • Capitalism causes rap, in responding to the market, to blunt its message; censorship
  • "success" defined in economic terms
  • white rap artists achieve greater "success" than black artists; white appropriation of black cultural forms
  • dialectic between marginalized and mainstream society
  • rap's reliance on independent labels as a form of resistance, but reincorporated into majority culture
  • rap as voice of disenfranchised culture
  • female rap artists' (and executives) struggle to overcome gender bias; female rappers' rap as resistance

Questions:

  • Property
  • Reproduction of art affecting value/perception/desire
  • Dissemination of ideology
  • Role of capitalism in perpetuating social inequality
  • Different voices of proletariat - how perceived by society
  • Distortion the occurs when monetary values are imposed on object that don't naturally lend themselves to that kind of valuation
  • Political contexts that give rise to subcultural forms
