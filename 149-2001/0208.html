Today:

  • Read John Perry Barlow's "A Declaration of Independence of Cyberspace"
  • Talk in discussion groups for about 15 minutes:
    • How would Benjamin/Adorno & Horkheimer respond to Barlow?
    • How about Althusser? Which of the essays seems more in keeping with "your" position?
    • Be specific in your reference to the essays. Be able to point directly to particular passages that illustrate the arguments you're interested in making.
  • Next, return to "debate teams": spend another 15 minutes talking among the groups about your position.
  • We'll return and hold a large group debate for the last 40 minutes of class. Make sure you bring any questions that you have about the articles, but be sure those questions are specific.

Adorno

  • Internet gives more access to info
  • but rampant capitalism
  • Barlow too idealistic in suggesting Internet is free
  • Internet requires access: computer, knowledge, etc.; Barlow is naïve in suggesting that all may enter without prejudice
  • The assimilation of resistance: the Internet is being assimilated as well -- move to corporatization of net

Benjamin

  • Barlow is right: internet represents new crisis; has potential to develop either way
  • Unlimited reproduction and distribution of Internet (infinite, no cost) annihilates the aura -- no longer any ritual value; may even begin to evade exchange value
  • Difference between paint & pixels; no original -- changes power dynamic between creator and viewer

Althusser

  • ISAs: molding ideology without violence; use of other media to assimilate what could be a form of resistance
  • Barlow's concern with governmental regulation misses the point: there's still ideological control
  • "free" internet access -- requires advertising; always being sold to
  • Internet will ultimately be an ISA
