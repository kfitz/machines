Discussion:

From The German Ideology:

  • Ideology should be based on "reality" and not on spiritual philosophy; can include abstractions, but must begin with material (German philosophy tended to start with abstractions)
  • The struggle is to find the connection between the abstract and the material
  • Man is responsible for and capable of his own subsistence in the formulation of ideas, truths, consciousness
  • The evolution of production and exchange represents our history best
  • History is retrojected based on the ideologies that currently exist

From The Eighteenth Brumaire:

  • The bourgeoisie cannot be trusted; any successful revolution must be a proletariat-based revolution
  • The petty-bourgeois is a transitional class used by the bourgeois against the proletariat; tricked into giving up their power
  • Whenever revolution happens, each involved group believes their mind-set is the right one; ideas are overthrown as ruling classes are
  • When staging a revolution, one shouldn't try to gain support of a more conservative group of individuals
  • The aims of the revolutionary individuals get diluted with each absorption into another group * Revolution was much more about material concerns than ideology
  • Merging of the landed bourgeois and the capitalists

From A Contribution to the Critique of Political Economy:

  • Only rarely are political groups capable of self-criticism; only they are capable of understanding the political economies of earlier times
  • When applying abstract concepts to other societies, must make sure they're not tainted with your own contexts; political economists are guilty of this, imposing bourgeois value systems on other types of economy (feudal, etc)
  • Criticizes other economists for starting with the abstract and working down to the concrete; need to begin with concrete

From Capital:

  • People are commodified by their labor; when they become commodities, they become exploitable
  • People become abstracted/alienated by treating their labor as a commodity
  • The reason for this alienation is that the value of a commodity is determined by the amount of labor that goes into it
  • Anti-industrialization; anti-mass-production
  • Differentiates between the quality and quantity of labor; capitalism does not

Connections:

  • Starting with ideals and moving to material is bad; must begin with material
  • Human nature: fundamentally negative; people need to be constrained; laissez-faire capitalism assumes goodness
  • Production of social classes and production of culture are tied to economic production
  • Interested in History
  • Interested in disentangling things that appear "natural" (ideology) from material reality

Questions:

  • Objects' value are determined by social reality, context

    • Exchange value: value determined by trade
    • Exchange value is bad because it relies on a system in which all are consumers; reflects the abstractions of this system
  • What does Marx make of the family/women in a communist system?
  • How much are Marx and what we later understand as Marxism/communism really related?
