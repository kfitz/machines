Today:

  1. Divide up into four groups by essay (all a's meet, all b's, etc.)
  2. Discuss your essay with an eye toward understanding it well enough to return to your discussion group and "teach" it to your peers.
  3. Spend about 30 minutes with your essay group, and then move into discussion groups.
  4. In discussion groups, introduce your essay to your group; spend 5 to 10 minutes outlining the argument for them.
  5. Begin to note connections among the four essays as you talk.
  6. Keep notes of specific questions that you need to have answered, either by your peers or by me.

For next time:

  1. Everybody reads the sections from Capital.
  2. Each of you should read one of the two remaining essays that you haven't yet read.
  3. Those of you who've already read the Capital essays should select two of the other three to read.
  4. Decide which essays you're going to read in consultation with your group - you may decide that all of you will focus on one of the essays that you understood least, or you may switch off essays so that each one is read at least twice.
  5. We'll start class next time in these small groups, talking for about fifteen or twenty minutes, after which we'll spend some time talking as a full class about the four essays.
